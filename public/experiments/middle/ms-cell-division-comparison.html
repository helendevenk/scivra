Key Formulas

Core Concepts

Mitosis produces two genetically identical diploid daughter cells (2n → 2n). It is used for growth, tissue repair, and asexual reproduction.

Meiosis produces four genetically unique haploid cells (2n → n). It occurs in reproductive organs and involves two sequential divisions with crossing over in Prophase I for genetic diversity.

Chromosomes condense in Prophase, align in Metaphase, separate in Anaphase, and reform nuclei in Telophase.

Real-World Applications

  • Cancer — uncontrolled mitotic cell division
  • Fertilization — haploid sperm + egg = diploid zygote
  • Stem cell therapy for tissue regeneration
  • Down syndrome — meiosis error (nondisjunction)
