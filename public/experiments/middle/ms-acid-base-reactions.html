Core Concepts

The pH scale measures acidity from 0 (most acidic) to 14 (most basic), with 7 being neutral. Each unit represents a 10-fold change in H⁺ concentration.

Acids donate H⁺ ions to solution, while bases donate OH⁻ ions. When mixed, H⁺ and OH⁻ combine to form neutral water (H₂O).

The water equilibrium constant Kw = 10⁻¹⁴ means that [H⁺] × [OH⁻] always equals 10⁻¹⁴ at 25°C, linking acid and base concentrations.

Real-World Applications

  • Stomach acid (pH 1-2) digesting food
  • Blood pH 7.35-7.45 for healthy function
  • Antacids neutralizing excess stomach acid
  • Soil pH management in agriculture
