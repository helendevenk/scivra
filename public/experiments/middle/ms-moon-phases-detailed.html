Solar vs Lunar Eclipse
Solar eclipse: Moon between Earth and Sun (new moon + alignment). Rare because Moon's orbit is tilted 5°.
Lunar eclipse: Earth's shadow on Moon (full moon + alignment). Moon appears red ("blood moon") from refracted sunlight.
Why Different Periods?
Sidereal (27.32 days) = time to complete one orbit relative to stars. Synodic (29.53 days) = longer because Earth also moved around the Sun, so Moon has to "catch up" to return to the same Sun-Earth-Moon alignment.
