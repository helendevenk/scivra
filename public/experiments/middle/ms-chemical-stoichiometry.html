Key Formulas
Mole Concept
A mole is Avogadro's number (6.022 × 10²³) of particles — atoms, molecules, or formula units. It allows chemists to count particles by weighing them, since one mole of any substance equals its molar mass in grams.
Stoichiometry uses balanced equations to predict how much product forms from given amounts of reactants. The coefficients give exact mole ratios between every species in the reaction.
Limiting Reagent
The limiting reagent is the reactant that is completely consumed first, stopping the reaction. The other reactant is in excess — some of it remains unreacted.
To find the limiting reagent: convert each reactant to moles of product using the mole ratio. The reactant that produces fewer moles of product is the limiting reagent.

Real-World Applications
Industrial Synthesis Haber-Bosch Process Combustion Engines Pharmaceutical Mfg Rocket Fuel Environmental Chem
The Haber process (N₂ + 3H₂ → 2NH₃) produces fertilizers that feed roughly half the world's population. Optimizing the limiting reagent ratio is central to its industrial efficiency.
