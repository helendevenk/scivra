Core Concepts

A Punnett square is a grid diagram that predicts the probability of offspring inheriting specific alleles. Each parent contributes one allele per gene to each offspring.

Dominant alleles (T) mask recessive alleles (t). Only individuals with two recessive alleles (tt) express the recessive phenotype — they cannot "hide" behind a dominant copy.

In a classic Tt × Tt monohybrid cross, offspring appear in a 3:1 phenotype ratio. The probability of any single offspring being recessive is exactly 25% (1/4).

Real-World Applications

  • Predicting genetic disease risk (cystic fibrosis, sickle cell)
  • Selective breeding in agriculture
  • Blood type inheritance and transfusion compatibility
  • Forensic DNA analysis and paternity testing
