Core Concepts

The epicenter is the point on Earth's surface directly above the earthquake's underground focus (hypocenter). Seismic waves radiate outward from the focus in all directions.

P-waves (compressional) travel fastest (~6 km/s) through rock. S-waves (shear) are slower (~3.5 km/s) and cannot travel through liquids. The time difference between P and S arrivals at a station reveals its distance from the epicenter.

Using triangulation from 3 stations, scientists draw circles with radii equal to each station's distance from the epicenter. Where all three circles intersect is the epicenter location.

Real-World Applications

  • Tsunami early warning systems
  • Building codes in seismic zones
  • Nuclear test monitoring (CTBTO)
  • Oil and gas exploration via controlled blasts
