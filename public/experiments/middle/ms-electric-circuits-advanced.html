Key Formulas
Ohm's Law & Resistance
Ohm's Law states that the current I through a conductor is directly proportional to the voltage V across it, provided temperature is constant. The proportionality constant R is the resistance in ohms (Ω).
Resistivity depends on material, length, and cross-sectional area: R = ρL/A. Metals have low resistivity; insulators have very high resistivity. Semiconductor resistivity decreases with temperature (opposite to metals).
Series vs Parallel
In a series circuit, all current flows through every component. R_total = R₁ + R₂ + ... Total resistance increases. Voltage divides across components.
In a parallel circuit, current splits between branches. 1/R_total = 1/R₁ + 1/R₂ + ... Total resistance decreases. All branches share the same voltage.
Household circuits are wired in parallel so each device receives full mains voltage and can be switched independently.
RC Circuits
When a capacitor charges through a resistor, voltage grows exponentially: V_C(t) = V₀(1 − e^(−t/RC)). The time constant τ = RC is the time to reach 63.2% of full charge. After 5τ, the capacitor is considered fully charged.
The glowing capacitor plates in the visualization show charge accumulating. The current decreases exponentially as the capacitor opposes further charging.
Real-World Applications
LED Lighting Power Distribution RC Filters Battery Charging Microprocessors Electric Vehicles
RC circuits form the basis of timing circuits in microcontrollers and signal filters in audio/radio electronics. The time constant determines which signal frequencies pass and which are attenuated.
