Energy Conservation PE ↔ KE · Work-Energy Theorem · Grade 6-8
Energy Formulas
Gravitational potential energy: depends on height above ground.
Kinetic energy: depends on speed squared — doubling speed quadruples KE!
Total energy is conserved (decreases slightly with friction).

Real World Example
A roller coaster converts PE→KE going downhill and KE→PE going uphill. The first hill is always the tallest — each subsequent hill must be lower because energy is lost to friction and air resistance.
Common Misconception
Energy is not destroyed by friction — it's converted to thermal energy (heat). Total energy of the universe is always conserved. We say energy is "lost" only from the mechanical system.
