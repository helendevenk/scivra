Core Concepts

When electronegativity difference (Δχ) > 1.7, electrons transfer completely from one atom to another, forming an ionic bond with strong electrostatic attraction.

When Δχ < 0.5, electrons are shared nearly equally in a nonpolar covalent bond. Between 0.5–1.7, the bond is polar covalent with unequal sharing.

Bond energy reflects how strongly atoms are held together — higher energy bonds are shorter and stronger. Dipole moment (D) quantifies charge separation direction.

Real-World Applications

  • Table salt (NaCl) — ionic crystal lattice
  • Water polarity enabling hydrogen bonds
  • Diamond hardness from strong C-C covalent bonds
  • Pharmaceutical drug binding sites

Bond Type Guide

Ionic: Δχ > 1.7
Polar covalent: 0.5–1.7
Nonpolar covalent: < 0.5
