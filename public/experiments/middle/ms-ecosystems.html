Core Concepts

The Lotka-Volterra model describes predator-prey cycles: when prey are abundant, predator populations grow; as predators increase, prey decline; then predators decline for lack of food, allowing prey to recover.

Carrying capacity (K) is the maximum population size an environment can sustain indefinitely, limited by food, water, space, and disease. Population growth slows as it approaches K (logistic growth).

Only ~10% of energy transfers between trophic levels. A lion requires ~100 kg of prey to gain 10 kg of biomass — explaining why food chains rarely exceed 4-5 levels.

Real-World Applications
