Reaction Types

Synthesis: A + B → AB (building up)
Decomposition: AB → A + B (breaking down)
Single Displacement: A + BC → AC + B
Double Displacement: AB + CD → AD + CB
Combustion: fuel + O₂ → CO₂ + H₂O

Exothermic vs Endothermic
Exothermic (ΔH < 0): releases heat energy → hand warmers, combustion, rusting iron. Endothermic (ΔH > 0): absorbs heat energy → ice packs, photosynthesis, cooking an egg.
Conservation of Mass
Atoms are never created or destroyed in a chemical reaction — they just rearrange into new compounds. Count atoms on both sides of the equation: they must be equal!
