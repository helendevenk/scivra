Crystal Systems

Cubic (pyrite, halite): 3 equal axes at 90°, high symmetry.
Hexagonal (quartz, calcite): 6-fold axis, forms prisms.
Tetragonal (zircon): 4-fold axis, elongated prisms.
Orthorhombic (olivine): 3 unequal axes at 90°.
Monoclinic (mica, gypsum): 2 axes at 90°, 1 oblique.
Triclinic (feldspar): no right angles, least symmetric.
Crystal habit: prismatic, tabular, bladed, fibrous, massive.

Identification Properties Guide

Color — least reliable; impurities cause wide variation
Streak — powder on unglazed ceramic; true diagnostic color
Luster — metallic, vitreous, resinous, pearly, silky, waxy, earthy
Hardness — Mohs 1–10; scratch test with known minerals
Cleavage — breaks along crystal planes (mica: 1 perfect)
Fracture — conchoidal (quartz), irregular, hackly, splintery
Specific Gravity — galena 7.6; quartz 2.65; feldspar 2.57

Real-World Applications

Quartz — oscillators in electronics, fiber optics, glass, abrasives. Feldspar — 60% of Earth's crust; ceramics, porcelain, glass. Pyrite — sulfuric acid production; historically confused with gold. Mica — electrical insulators, capacitors, paint, cosmetics. Diamond — cutting tools, drill bits, quantum computing.
