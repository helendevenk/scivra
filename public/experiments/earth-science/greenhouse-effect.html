Greenhouse Notes

Natural greenhouse effect: Without any greenhouse gases, Earth's average temperature would be −18°C instead of +15°C — a 33°C warming. Life depends on this baseline effect.

GHG absorption spectra: CO₂ absorbs IR at 15 μm, H₂O at 6.3 μm and far-IR, CH₄ at 3.3 and 7.7 μm, N₂O at 4.5 and 7.8 μm. Together they cover most of the outgoing IR spectrum.

Anthropogenic forcing: CO₂ increased from 280 ppm (pre-industrial) to 420 ppm (2024) — highest in 800,000 years of ice core records. Rate of increase: ~2.5 ppm/year.

Knowledge Cards
Water Vapor Feedback
Most powerful GHG. A positive feedback: warming → more evaporation → more H₂O vapor → more warming. Amplifies CO₂ forcing by ~2×. Controlled by temperature (Clausius-Clapeyron equation).
Ice-Albedo Feedback
Ice reflects 80–90% of sunlight (albedo ~0.85). Ocean absorbs 93% (albedo ~0.07). As Arctic ice melts, darker ocean replaces it → more solar absorption → more warming → more melting. Runaway amplification.
Radiative Forcing
CO₂ doubling: ΔF = 5.35 × ln(2) ≈ 3.7 W/m². Equilibrium climate sensitivity (ECS) = 2.5–4°C per doubling. Current best estimate: 3°C (IPCC AR6, 2021).
Why It Matters

The greenhouse effect is the central mechanism of anthropogenic climate change. Understanding the forcing equations and feedback loops is necessary to evaluate climate model projections, carbon budgets (remaining emissions to stay under 1.5°C or 2°C), and policy decisions on energy transitions.
