Tectonic Notes

Oceanic-continental subduction: Dense oceanic plate dips beneath continental crust → Andes-type volcanic arcs. Wadati-Benioff zone reaches 700 km depth. Generates M9+ megathrust earthquakes.

Continental-continental collision: When oceanic plate is consumed, continents collide → Himalayas. India-Asia convergence at 5 cm/yr, ongoing. Crust thickened to ~70 km.

Wilson Cycle: 500–600 Myr cycle from rifting to supercontinent collision. Pangaea ~300 Ma → breakup ~200 Ma → future Pangaea Proxima in ~250 Myr.

Knowledge Cards
San Andreas Fault
Transform boundary, 1,300 km long. Pacific Plate moves NW at ~5 cm/yr relative to North American Plate. 1906 San Francisco earthquake (M7.9). Locks and loads every ~150 years.
Mid-Atlantic Ridge
Divergent, spreading at ~2.5 cm/yr. N. America and Europe drifting apart. Iceland: only place the ridge emerges above sea level. Active volcanoes + geysers + hydrothermal vents.
Pacific Ring of Fire
Basin rim — 75% of world's volcanoes, 90% of earthquakes. Driven by subduction of Pacific, Cocos, Juan de Fuca, Nazca, Philippine plates beneath surrounding continents.
Why It Matters

Plate tectonics is the unifying theory of Earth science. It explains volcanic arcs, earthquake distributions, mountain chains, ocean basin formation, and metallic ore deposit locations. Understanding boundary types directly informs seismic hazard maps, building codes, and tsunami warning systems in countries like Japan, Indonesia, and Chile.
