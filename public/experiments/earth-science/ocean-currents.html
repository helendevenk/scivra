Thermohaline Circulation

The thermohaline circulation (THC) is driven by differences in water temperature (thermo) and salinity (haline), which together control ocean density. Cold, salty water is denser and sinks in the North Atlantic (deep water formation), while warm surface water flows poleward to replace it. This creates the global ocean conveyor belt that transports heat around the planet, regulating climate — especially in Europe and coastal regions.

Major Ocean Currents

Gulf Stream — warm western boundary current, Atlantic
North Atlantic Drift — keeps NW Europe mild
Kuroshio Current — warm western Pacific current
California Current — cold eastern boundary current
Antarctic Circumpolar — strongest current on Earth
Peru/Humboldt Current — cold, upwelling-rich

Upwelling & Downwelling

Upwelling: wind drives surface water away from coast; cold, nutrient-rich deep water rises. Creates productive fisheries (Peru, California). Downwelling: surface water converges and sinks; transports oxygen to deep ocean but limits surface nutrients.

Real-World Impact

Ocean currents moderate climate by redistributing ~40% of the Sun's energy absorbed by the oceans. Disruption of THC due to freshwater influx (melting ice) could cause rapid climate shifts. El Niño/La Niña are temporary disruptions of normal Pacific current patterns affecting global weather.
