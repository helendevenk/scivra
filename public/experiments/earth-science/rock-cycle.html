Rock Types

Igneous — formed from cooling magma/lava; granite (intrusive), basalt (extrusive)
Sedimentary — formed from compacted sediments; sandstone, limestone, shale
Metamorphic — transformed by heat and pressure; marble (from limestone), gneiss (from granite)

Rock Cycle Pathways

Igneous → Sedimentary: weathering + erosion + deposition + lithification
Sedimentary → Metamorphic: burial + heat + pressure (no melting)
Metamorphic → Igneous: melting to magma + cooling/crystallization
Any rock → Magma: subduction + melting at high T
Any rock → Sedimentary: uplift + weathering + transport
