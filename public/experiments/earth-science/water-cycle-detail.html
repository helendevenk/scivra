Solar radiation (343 W/m²) drives the entire hydrological cycle. ~80% of evaporation comes from oceans. Plants contribute ~10% via transpiration. The atmosphere holds ~13,000 km³ of water at any time.

Evaporation & Transpiration

Evaporation occurs when liquid water absorbs enough energy to overcome intermolecular forces (latent heat = 2,257 kJ/kg). Rate increases with temperature, wind speed, and low humidity. Oceans evaporate ~434,000 km³/year.

Cloud Formation & Precipitation

Water vapor rises, cools adiabatically (−9.8°C/km dry, −6°C/km saturated). At dew point, condensation nuclei (dust, sea salt) allow droplet formation. Rain requires droplets to coalesce to ~2 mm. Snow forms below 0°C; hail forms in strong updrafts.

Groundwater & Aquifers

Infiltration rate depends on soil porosity and permeability. Water percolates through the vadose zone to the water table. Confined aquifers (artesian) are bounded by impermeable rock. Residence time: days (streams) to thousands of years (deep aquifers).

Why It Matters

The water cycle distributes heat globally (latent heat transport), shapes climate, drives erosion, and supports all terrestrial life. Human disruption (groundwater depletion, deforestation, urbanization) affects precipitation patterns and drought risk.
