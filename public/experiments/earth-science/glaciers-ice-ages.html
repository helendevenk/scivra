Glacier Notes

Formation: Snowflake → firn (partially compacted, density 0.4–0.83 g/cm³) → glacial ice (density 0.917 g/cm³, takes 10–1000 years). Air bubbles trapped in ice preserve ancient atmospheric samples.

Erosional features: Cirques (bowl-shaped basins), arêtes (knife-edge ridges), horns (pyramid peaks like the Matterhorn), U-shaped valleys, fjords (flooded U-valleys), drumlins, eskers, moraines.

Milankovitch cycles: Eccentricity 100,000 yr → obliquity 41,000 yr → precession 23,000 yr. Together modulate summer insolation at 65°N — key trigger for ice age initiation and termination.

Knowledge Cards
Last Glacial Maximum (LGM)
~21,000 years ago. Ice sheets covered North America to ~40°N (Laurentide, 3 km thick), Europe, and Antarctica. Sea level ~120 m lower than today. Sahara was cold and dry, not hot and dry.
Antarctic Ice Core Records
EPICA Dome C core (Antarctica): 800,000-year record of 8 glacial-interglacial cycles. CO₂ ranged 180–300 ppm over this period — tightly coupled to temperature. CO₂ today (420 ppm) is unprecedented in this record.
Current Arctic Sea Ice
Arctic summer sea ice extent has declined ~13% per decade since 1979. Ice-free Arctic summer possible by 2040s. Triggers ice-albedo feedback: positive amplification of global warming.
Why It Matters

Ice ages shaped human migration, sea level changes of 120+ meters, and current landscape morphology (Great Lakes formed by glacial erosion). Ice cores are Earth's thermometer for 800,000 years. Modern glacier retreat threatens freshwater supply for billions (Himalayas, Andes glaciers feed major rivers in dry seasons).
