Wave Equations

Seismic Wave Types

P-waves (Primary) are compressional body waves — particles move parallel to wave propagation. They travel through all Earth layers (solid + liquid) at 5–8 km/s in crust, reaching seismographs first.

S-waves (Secondary) are shear body waves — particles move perpendicular to propagation. They travel only through solid material at 3–5 km/s, and are blocked by Earth's liquid outer core.

The S-wave shadow zone (103°–142° from epicenter) proved Earth has a liquid outer core. P-wave velocity changes at layer boundaries produce refraction, bending wave paths like light through glass.

Shadow Zones
P-wave shadow: 103°–142° (refraction by liquid core). S-wave shadow: >103° (S-waves cannot travel through liquid outer core). These gaps in seismograph readings worldwide reveal Earth's internal structure.
Earthquake Location
The P-S wave arrival time difference (t_S − t_P) determines distance to epicenter. With 3+ seismograph stations, triangulation pinpoints the epicenter. Each 1 second difference ≈ ~8 km distance.

Why It Matters — NGSS ESS2.A

Seismology reveals Earth's internal structure without drilling. Wave velocity anomalies map mantle convection, subduction zones, and hotspots. Understanding seismic waves is critical for earthquake engineering and tsunami warning systems.
