Concept

Reaction rate depends on collision frequency and collision energy. Only collisions with energy ≥ Ea (activation energy) AND correct orientation lead to products. Temperature dramatically increases both factors.

Catalysts provide an alternative pathway with lower Ea, increasing k without being consumed. A 10 K increase roughly doubles the rate (for typical Ea ≈ 50 kJ/mol).

Rate Order Summary
0th order: [A]t = [A]₀ - kt
1st order: ln[A]t = -kt + ln[A]₀ → t½ = ln2/k
2nd order: 1/[A]t = kt + 1/[A]₀
Why It Matters
Kinetics determines how fast drugs act in the body, industrial processes are optimized, and environmental pollutants degrade. Catalytic converters work by lowering Ea for combustion byproducts.
