Half-Reactions
Concept

In a galvanic (voltaic) cell, spontaneous redox reactions generate electrical energy. The anode (oxidation) loses electrons; the cathode (reduction) gains them. Electrons flow through the external circuit; ions flow through the salt bridge to maintain electrical neutrality.

The Nernst equation corrects E° for non-standard concentrations. When Q=1, E=E°. When Q>1 (more products), E decreases. At equilibrium, E=0 and Q=K.

AP Exam Tip
Higher (more positive) E°cell = more spontaneous = larger K = more negative ΔG°. Faraday's constant F = 96485 C/mol. For Zn/Cu: n=2, E°=1.10V.
Why It Matters
Batteries, fuel cells, corrosion, electroplating, and chlor-alkali electrolysis all use electrochemistry. Li-ion batteries power phones and EVs using lithium intercalation half-reactions.
