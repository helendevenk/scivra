ΔG Analysis
Concept

Gibbs free energy ΔG determines spontaneity at constant T and P. A process is spontaneous when ΔG < 0 — it releases useful work. ΔH captures the enthalpy (heat) change; TΔS captures the entropy (disorder) contribution.

When ΔH < 0 and ΔS > 0 (exothermic, more disorder), the reaction is always spontaneous. When ΔH > 0 and ΔS < 0, never spontaneous. Other combinations depend on temperature.

Spontaneity Table
ΔH− ΔS+: always spontaneous
ΔH+ ΔS−: never spontaneous
ΔH− ΔS−: spontaneous at low T
ΔH+ ΔS+: spontaneous at high T
Why It Matters
Thermodynamics predicts whether reactions, batteries, industrial processes, and biological reactions can occur spontaneously. ΔG° connects to K and cell potential in electrochemistry.
