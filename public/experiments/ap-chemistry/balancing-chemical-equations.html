Atom Balance
Concept

Antoine Lavoisier's Law of Conservation of Mass (1789): matter is neither created nor destroyed in a chemical reaction. Balancing equations ensures the same number of each atom appears on both sides, reflecting this law at the atomic level.

A balanced equation is the foundation of all stoichiometric calculations — it defines the mole ratios used to calculate yields, limiting reagents, and percent composition.

AP Exam Tip
Balance equations by inspection: start with elements that appear in fewest compounds, balance polyatomic ions as units, and leave H and O for last. Never change subscripts — only coefficients.
Why It Matters
Every AP Chemistry calculation (stoichiometry, limiting reagent, gas laws) depends on a balanced equation. It is the single most tested skill in AP Chem FRQs.
