VSEPR: electron pairs repel to maximize separation

Concept

Lewis dot structures represent valence electrons as dots around atomic symbols. Shared pairs become bonds (lines), and lone (nonbonding) pairs remain as dots. The goal: every atom achieves an octet (except H which wants 2).

Formal charge = (valence electrons) − (nonbonding electrons) − ½(bonding electrons). The best Lewis structure minimizes formal charges on all atoms and places negative formal charge on the most electronegative atom.

VSEPR theory predicts geometry: electron pairs (bonding + lone) arrange to minimize repulsion. Lone pairs repel more than bonding pairs, compressing bond angles below ideal values.

VSEPR Geometries
2 e-groups: linear (180°)
3 e-groups: trigonal planar (120°), bent (≈117°)
4 e-groups: tetrahedral (109.5°), trigonal pyramidal (107°), bent (104.5°)
Octet Exceptions
BF₃ (only 6e on B), PCl₅ and SF₆ (expanded octet using d orbitals), NO (odd-electron radical).
