Le Chatelier

When a system at equilibrium is disturbed, it shifts to oppose the change:

  • • Add reactant → shifts right (→)
  • • Add product → shifts left (←)
  • • Increase T (endothermic) → shifts right
  • • Increase pressure → shifts toward fewer moles of gas
Q vs K Rules
Q < K → reaction proceeds forward (→)
Q > K → reaction proceeds backward (←)
Q = K → at equilibrium
Why It Matters
The Haber process for NH₃ synthesis uses Le Chatelier's principle: high pressure (more reactant moles → right) and low temperature (exothermic → right) to maximize yield.
