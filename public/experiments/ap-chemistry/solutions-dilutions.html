Solutions & Dilutions Molarity, Dissolving & Dilution

Core Formulas

Molarity: moles of solute per liter of solution

Dilution equation: moles of solute are conserved

Ksp: solubility product for sparingly soluble salts

Concept

Molarity (M) is the most common concentration unit in AP Chemistry: M = moles of solute / liters of solution. A 1.0 M NaCl solution contains 1 mole of NaCl (58.44 g) dissolved to make exactly 1 liter of solution.

When you dilute a solution, you add solvent but the moles of solute stay the same. C₁V₁ = C₂V₂ captures this: if you start with 100 mL of 1.0 M solution and dilute to 1000 mL, the new concentration is 0.1 M (10-fold dilution).

Ion concentration in solution drives colligative properties (boiling point elevation, freezing point depression, osmotic pressure) and determines solution color for transition metal complexes like CuSO₄ (blue, Cu²⁺) and KMnO₄ (purple, MnO₄⁻).

Common Lab Solutions
NaCl brine: 0.9% ≈ 0.15 M (physiological)
CuSO₄: blue color, [Cu²⁺] determines intensity
KMnO₄: deep purple, used as oxidizing agent
HCl 1M: standard acid titrant
Ksp Examples
NaCl: soluble (no Ksp needed)
AgCl: Ksp = 1.8×10⁻¹⁰ (sparingly soluble)
CaCO₃: Ksp = 3.3×10⁻⁹
