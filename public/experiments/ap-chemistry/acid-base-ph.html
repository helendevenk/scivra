Concept

Strong acids (HCl, H₂SO₄, HNO₃) dissociate completely — [H⁺] = initial concentration. Weak acids partially dissociate according to their Ka value. A buffer resists pH changes because it contains both a weak acid and its conjugate base.

Titration curves show pH vs. volume of titrant added. At the equivalence point, moles of acid = moles of base. The half-equivalence point equals pKa for a weak acid.

AP Exam Tip
pH + pOH = 14 at 25°C. For a strong acid: pH = -log(C). For a weak acid: pH = ½(pKa - log C). Buffer capacity is maximum at pH = pKa.
Why It Matters
Blood pH (7.35–7.45) is maintained by the carbonic acid/bicarbonate buffer. Ocean acidification occurs as CO₂ dissolves, lowering pH. Antacids neutralize stomach acid (HCl, pH ≈ 1.5).
