Electron Configuration Aufbau, Pauli & Hund's Rules

Core Rules

Aufbau: fill lowest energy orbitals first

Pauli: no two electrons can have identical quantum numbers

Hund: maximize unpaired electrons in degenerate orbitals

Concept

Three quantum mechanical rules govern how electrons fill atomic orbitals. The Aufbau principle fills orbitals from lowest to highest energy (1s first, then 2s, 2p, 3s...). The diagonal rule (n+l) determines filling order.

Pauli Exclusion: each orbital holds at most 2 electrons, and they must have opposite spins (↑↓). This is because no two fermions can share all four quantum numbers (n, l, ml, ms).

Hund's Rule: when filling degenerate orbitals (same energy, e.g., 2px, 2py, 2pz), place one electron in each before doubling up. This minimizes electron-electron repulsion.

Orbital Box Notation
↑ = spin up (ms=+½), ↓ = spin down (ms=−½). Each box represents one orbital. Paired electrons always show ↑↓ together.
Exceptions
Cr ([Ar]3d⁵4s¹) and Cu ([Ar]3d¹⁰4s¹) violate simple Aufbau — half-filled and fully-filled d subshells have extra stability.
AP Chem Connection
Electron config determines chemical properties: valence electrons drive bonding, oxidation states, and periodic trends in ionization energy and electronegativity.
