Concept

Stoichiometry uses balanced equations to calculate the amounts of reactants consumed and products formed. The limiting reagent (limiting reactant) is the substance that is entirely consumed first, determining the maximum yield.

The excess reagent remains after the reaction is complete. Percent yield compares actual experimental yield to the theoretical maximum.

Common Misconception
The limiting reagent is NOT always the one with the smaller mass or fewer moles — it depends on the mole ratio from the balanced equation.
Why It Matters
Industrial synthesis, pharmaceutical manufacturing, and environmental chemistry all depend on stoichiometric calculations to minimize waste and maximize product yield.
