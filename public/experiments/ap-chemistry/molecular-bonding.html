Molecular Bonding Ionic vs Covalent Bonds

Core Formulas

ΔEN > 1.7 → ionic; 0.5–1.7 → polar covalent; < 0.5 → nonpolar covalent

Lattice energy: energy to separate 1 mol ionic compound into gaseous ions

Bond energy inversely related to bond length; triple > double > single

Concept

Chemical bonds form when atoms lower their potential energy by sharing or transferring electrons. The electronegativity difference (ΔEN) between bonded atoms determines bond character: large ΔEN → electron transfer → ionic bond; small ΔEN → electron sharing → covalent bond.

Ionic compounds (NaCl) form crystal lattices — Na donates its 3s electron to Cl's 3p, creating Na⁺ and Cl⁻ ions held together by electrostatic attraction. Lattice energy can exceed 800 kJ/mol — extremely stable.

Covalent bonds form when atoms share electrons. N₂ has a triple bond (very short, 110 pm, very strong 945 kJ/mol). H₂O has polar covalent O-H bonds (ΔEN = 1.4) with partial charges δ+ on H and δ− on O.
