Concept

Kinematics describes how objects move without asking why. The three graphs — position, velocity, and acceleration vs. time — are different windows onto the same motion.

The slope of the x-t graph gives velocity. The slope of the v-t graph gives acceleration. The area under the v-t graph gives displacement. These relationships are the backbone of AP Physics kinematics.

Constant acceleration produces a parabolic x-t curve, a straight v-t line, and a horizontal a-t line. When acceleration is zero, x-t is linear and v-t is flat.

Key Insights
Graph Slopes
Slope of x-t = v; slope of v-t = a. If x-t is curved (parabola), the object is accelerating.
Common Misconception
At the highest point of a thrown ball, velocity is zero — but acceleration is still −9.8 m/s². Gravity never turns off.
AP Connection
AP Physics 1 Exam regularly asks students to match x-t, v-t, and a-t graphs for the same motion scenario.
Why It Matters

• Automotive safety engineers use kinematics to calculate stopping distances and design crumple zones.

• Space agencies use kinematic equations to plan rocket trajectories and landing profiles.
