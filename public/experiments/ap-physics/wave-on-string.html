Wave on a String AP Physics — Mechanical Waves
Core Formulas
Concept
A wave on a string transfers energy through coupled oscillators. Each mass point is pulled toward equilibrium by tension from its neighbors — like a chain of tiny pendulums.
Wave speed depends only on tension T and linear mass density μ. Doubling tension increases speed by √2 — not double. This surprises most students.
Standing waves form when the string length is a half-integer multiple of the wavelength. Nodes are fixed points; antinodes vibrate with maximum amplitude.
Key Facts
Standing Waves: Nodes occur at multiples of λ/2 from a fixed end. The 1st harmonic has exactly one antinode.
Common Misconception: Wave speed ≠ particle speed. The string particles oscillate transversely; the wave pattern moves horizontally.
AP Connection: AP Physics 1 Unit 10 — Mechanical Waves. Know v=√(T/μ), standing wave conditions, and energy proportional to A².
Why It Matters
  • Guitar/violin strings — tuning by changing tension
  • Seismic S-waves travel through Earth's mantle
  • Resonance in bridges (Tacoma Narrows collapse, 1940)
