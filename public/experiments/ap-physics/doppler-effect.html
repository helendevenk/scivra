Concept
When a sound source moves, wavefronts pile up ahead of it (shorter λ → higher f) and stretch out behind (longer λ → lower f). The observer hears a higher pitch as the source approaches and a lower pitch as it recedes.
At Mach 1 the source travels at the wave speed — wavefronts pile up into a shock cone. Above Mach 1 (supersonic) the source outruns its own wavefronts, creating a sonic boom.
History: Christian Doppler described this in 1842. Confirmed with trumpeters on a moving train by Buys Ballot (1845).
Misconception: "The source frequency changes." No — only the observed frequency changes. The source always emits f_s.
AP Link: AP Physics B/C: know the sign convention for approaching vs receding source and observer separately.
Why It Matters
  • • Police radar guns measure vehicle speed via Doppler shift
  • • Astronomers measure galactic recession via redshift
  • • Medical ultrasound uses Doppler to measure blood flow velocity
