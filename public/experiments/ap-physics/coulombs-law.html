Concept

Coulomb's Law describes the electrostatic force between two point charges. Like gravity, it follows an inverse-square law — doubling the distance cuts the force to one-quarter.

The sign of the charges determines force direction: same-sign charges repel, opposite-sign charges attract.

This is the foundation of all electrostatics — from the structure of atoms to the operation of capacitors.

Did You Know?
Historical Fact
Charles-Augustin de Coulomb measured this law using a torsion balance in 1785 — 92 years before the electron was discovered!
Common Misconception
The charges in Coulomb's Law must be treated as point charges or spherically symmetric — extended charge distributions require integration.
AP Connection
