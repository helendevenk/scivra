Quick Quiz
1. If the distance between two charges is doubled, the electrostatic force:
A) Doubles
B) Halves
C) Becomes one-quarter
D) Stays the same
Correct! F ∝ 1/r², so doubling r reduces F by a factor of 2² = 4. This is the inverse-square law.
2. Two charges of +3 μC and -3 μC are placed 0.1 m apart. The force is:
A) Repulsive, 8.1 N
B) Attractive, 8.1 N
C) Attractive, 81 N
D) Repulsive, 0.81 N
Correct! Opposite charges attract. F = k|q₁||q₂|/r² = 8.99×10⁹ × 3×10⁻⁶ × 3×10⁻⁶ / (0.1)² ≈ 8.1 N.
