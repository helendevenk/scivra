Newton's Laws
First Law (Inertia): An object at rest stays at rest, and an object in motion stays in motion with constant velocity, unless acted upon by a net external force. If applied force equals friction, the object moves at constant velocity (or stays still).
Second Law (F=ma): The acceleration of an object is directly proportional to the net force and inversely proportional to its mass. A larger net force produces greater acceleration; a heavier object accelerates less for the same force. The friction force f = μN = μmg opposes motion.
