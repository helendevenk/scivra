Newton's Laws
\(\vec{F}_{net} = m\vec{a}\)
Newton's 2nd Law: net force equals mass times acceleration
\(a = \frac{F_{net}}{m} = \frac{F - f}{m}\)
Acceleration from applied force minus friction
\(f_k = \mu_k N \quad f_s \leq \mu_s N\)
Kinetic and static friction. µ_s > µ_k always.
Inclined Plane
\(F_\parallel = mg\sin\theta\)
Component along slope (causes motion down)
\(N = mg\cos\theta\)
Normal force (perpendicular to slope)
\(a = g(\sin\theta - \mu_k\cos\theta)\)
Net acceleration down the slope with friction
Atwood Machine
\(a = \frac{(m_2 - m_1)g}{m_1 + m_2}\)
Acceleration when masses differ (massless string/pulley)
\(T = \frac{2m_1 m_2 g}{m_1 + m_2}\)
Tension in string connecting the two masses
Free Body Diagram Tips
Always Draw FBD First
List all forces: weight mg↓, normal N⊥surface, friction f‖surface (opposes motion), tension T along string, applied force F in direction of push/pull.
Choose Smart Axes
For inclined planes: tilt x-axis along slope. This makes N and component decompositions cleaner — fewer trig terms in each equation.
