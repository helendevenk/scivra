Concept Guide
Static friction prevents motion up to a maximum value fs,max = μsN. Once the net force exceeds this threshold, the object begins sliding and kinetic friction takes over at a lower constant value fk = μkN.

On an inclined plane, gravity splits into a component mg sinθ pulling the object down the slope and mg cosθ pressing it into the surface. The critical angle occurs when tanθ = μs.
