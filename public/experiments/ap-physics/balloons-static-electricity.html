CONCEPT
When you rub a balloon against wool, electrons transfer from wool to balloon — balloon becomes negatively charged, wool becomes positively charged.

Near a wall, the balloon's electric field polarizes nearby atoms — pushing electrons away, leaving a net positive surface charge. This induced charge attracts the balloon even though the wall is overall neutral.

On humid days, water molecules in air conduct charge away. The balloon loses charge faster than you can accumulate it — that's why static electricity is weaker in summer.
DID YOU KNOW
⚡ Lightning is static electricity on a massive scale — charge separation in storm clouds reaches millions of volts.
⚠️ Misconception: balloons don't "pick up" positive charge from wool — they gain electrons (negative). The deficit of electrons makes wool positive.

WHY IT MATTERS
  • Photocopiers use static charge to transfer toner to paper
  • Air purifiers use electrostatic precipitators to remove particles
  • ESD protection in electronics manufacturing (static kills chips)
