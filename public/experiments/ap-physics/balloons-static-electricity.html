Quick Quiz
1. When you rub a balloon on a sweater, the balloon becomes negatively charged because:
A) Protons move from the sweater to the balloon
B) Electrons transfer from the sweater to the balloon
C) The balloon creates new negative charges
D) The sweater destroys positive charges
Correct! Charge is not created or destroyed. Rubbing transfers electrons from the sweater to the balloon via the triboelectric effect, making the balloon negative and the sweater equally positive.
2. A negatively charged balloon sticks to a neutral wall because:
A) The wall becomes negatively charged
B) Gravity pulls the balloon toward the wall
C) The balloon induces a positive surface charge on the wall
D) The balloon and wall have equal and opposite charges
Correct! The negative balloon repels electrons in the wall's surface, inducing a net positive charge on the near side. This polarization creates an attractive Coulomb force, even though the wall is electrically neutral overall.
