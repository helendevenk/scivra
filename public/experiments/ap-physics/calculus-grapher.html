Core Formulas
Derivative: instantaneous rate of change (slope of tangent)
Definite integral: signed area under the curve
Fundamental Theorem of Calculus: derivative and integral are inverses
Chain rule for composite functions
Concept

The derivative f'(x) gives the slope of the tangent line to f(x) at each point. Where f(x) has a peak or valley, f'(x) = 0. Where f(x) rises steeply, f'(x) is large and positive.

The integral ∫f(x)dx accumulates the signed area under f(x). It grows when f(x) > 0 and shrinks when f(x) < 0. The Fundamental Theorem of Calculus says differentiation and integration are inverses.

Knowledge Cards
Physics connection: If f(x) is position, then f'(x) is velocity and ∫f(x)dx relates to displacement. Calculus is the language of all physics equations.
Inflection points: Where f'(x) changes sign, f(x) has a local max or min. Where f''(x) = 0 (f'(x) has a min/max), f(x) has an inflection point — where curvature reverses.
AP connection: AP Physics C requires calculus for kinematics, work integrals, and differential equations for circuits and oscillations. Understanding these graphs is essential.
Why It Matters
Every differential equation in physics (Newton's 2nd Law, Schrödinger equation) requires understanding derivatives.
Work = ∫F·dx and Impulse = ∫F·dt are integrals. Area under velocity-time graph = displacement.
Signal processing, machine learning (backpropagation), and engineering optimization all depend on calculus.
