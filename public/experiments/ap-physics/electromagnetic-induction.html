Concept
When a magnet moves through a coil, the changing magnetic flux induces an EMF. The minus sign in Faraday's law encodes Lenz's law: the induced current creates a field that opposes the change in flux — it always fights the cause.
Key insight: only changing flux matters. A stationary magnet inside the coil induces zero EMF. Peak EMF occurs when the magnet is at the coil plane (dΦ/dt is maximum), not when it's centered.
Knowledge Cards
⚡ Faraday 1831 — Discovered induction simultaneously with Henry. Led directly to electric generators and transformers.
⚠️ Misconception — Maximum B inside the coil does NOT mean maximum EMF. Max EMF occurs at maximum dB/dt, not maximum B.
📡 AP Connection — AP Physics C E&M Unit 4. Faraday's Law is essential for calculating transformer EMF ratios (V₁/V₂ = N₁/N₂).
Why It Matters
• Power generators — spinning coils in magnetic fields
• Wireless phone charging (Qi) — oscillating B induces EMF
• Guitar pickups — vibrating string changes flux in coil
