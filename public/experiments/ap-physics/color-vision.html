Concept

Human vision relies on three cone types sensitive to Long (red, ~560 nm), Medium (green, ~530 nm), and Short (blue, ~430 nm) wavelengths. Any perceived color is a combination of these three signals.

Additive mixing (light): Red + Green = Yellow; Green + Blue = Cyan; Red + Blue = Magenta; all three = White. Used in displays and projectors.

Subtractive mixing (pigments): absorbs certain wavelengths. CMY inks remove R, G, B respectively from white light.

Knowledge Cards

Historical: Thomas Young proposed the trichromatic theory of color vision in 1802, later extended by Helmholtz and Maxwell.
Misconception: Mixing red and green paint makes brown — not yellow. Paints use subtractive mixing. Light uses additive mixing.
AP Connection: AP Physics 1 covers visible spectrum (400–700 nm), wave speed c = fλ, and the electromagnetic spectrum ordering by frequency.

Why It Matters

  • Display technology (OLED/LCD) — pixel-level RGB mixing
  • Photography and cinema — color grading uses additive model
  • Medical imaging — false-color overlays aid diagnosis
  • AP Physics — Electromagnetic Spectrum Unit
