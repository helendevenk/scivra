Quiz
1. Why does rubbing your feet on carpet cause charge buildup?
A) Heat from friction creates new charges
B) Electrons transfer between materials due to differences in electron affinity (triboelectric effect)
C) Protons move from the carpet to your body
D) The carpet generates an electric field that pushes charges into you
Correct! The triboelectric effect causes electron transfer between two materials rubbed together. Certain materials (like carpet) tend to lose electrons, while others (like skin/socks) tend to gain them. No new charges are created -- they are only transferred.
2. What determines when a spark will jump from your finger to a doorknob?
A) The total charge on your body exceeds a fixed limit
B) The electric field between finger and knob exceeds the dielectric breakdown strength of air (~30 kV/cm)
C) Your body temperature rises enough to ionize the air
D) The doorknob must be made of a magnetic material
Correct! A spark (discharge) occurs when the electric field strength between the charged body and a grounded conductor exceeds air's dielectric breakdown strength of approximately 30 kV/cm. The gap distance and accumulated voltage both matter.
