Hooke's Law Explained
Hooke's Law states that the force needed to extend or compress a spring is directly proportional to the displacement from its natural length, as long as the elastic limit is not exceeded.
Beyond the elastic limit, the material deforms permanently (plastic deformation) and the linear relationship breaks down. The spring constant k measures stiffness: higher k means a stiffer spring.
Real-World Applications
  • Vehicle suspension systems
  • Mechanical watches & clocks
  • Trampolines and mattresses
  • Archery bows
  • Seismometers for earthquake detection
  • Force gauges and spring scales
