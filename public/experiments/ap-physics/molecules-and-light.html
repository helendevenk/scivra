Concept

Atoms exist in quantized energy states. When an electron falls from a higher level (n₂) to a lower level (n₁), it emits a photon with exactly the energy difference ΔE = E₂ − E₁ = hf.

This produces a unique emission spectrum — like a fingerprint — used to identify atoms in stars, galaxies, and flames. Absorption spectroscopy reverses the process: atoms absorb photons at the same wavelengths they emit.

The Balmer series of hydrogen falls in the visible range (410–656 nm), while the Lyman series is UV and Paschen is infrared.

Knowledge Cards

Historical: In 1885 Balmer found a simple formula for the 4 visible hydrogen lines. Bohr's 1913 model explained WHY using quantized angular momentum.
Misconception: Electrons do not "orbit" in definite paths. The Bohr model is an approximation; electrons occupy probabilistic orbitals described by quantum mechanics.
AP Connection: AP Physics Modern section — photoelectric effect, atomic models, line spectra, E=hf. Frequent FRQ topic in AP Physics 2.

Why It Matters

  • Stellar spectroscopy reveals composition, temperature, and velocity of stars
  • Fluorescent lamps and LEDs exploit atomic transitions
  • MRI uses proton spin-flip transitions at radio frequencies
