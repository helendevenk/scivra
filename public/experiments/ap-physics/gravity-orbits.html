Concept

Gravity provides the centripetal force that keeps planets in orbit. The exact orbit shape — circular, elliptical, or hyperbolic — depends entirely on the initial speed relative to the circular velocity v_c = √(GM/r).

Kepler's 3rd Law (T² ∝ a³) means any two planets around the same star obey the same ratio T²/a³, regardless of mass. This is a direct consequence of Newton's gravity.

At exactly v_esc = √2 · v_c, total energy = 0 and the orbit becomes parabolic — the threshold between bound and unbound. Above that speed, the trajectory is hyperbolic.

Knowledge Cards

Historical Fact

Kepler discovered T² ∝ a³ in 1619 purely from data — without knowing why. Newton derived it from his gravity law 50 years later, proving the inverse-square form.

Common Misconception

Planets do NOT slow down to conserve energy in elliptical orbits — they slow down to conserve angular momentum. Energy is constant throughout the orbit.

Advanced Connection

Kepler's 2nd law (equal areas in equal times) is equivalent to conservation of angular momentum — a consequence of the central nature of gravity, not its specific strength.
