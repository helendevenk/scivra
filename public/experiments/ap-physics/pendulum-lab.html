Simple Harmonic Motion
A simple pendulum exhibits approximately simple harmonic motion for small angles. The restoring torque is proportional to the angular displacement.

The period depends only on string length and gravity — it is independent of mass and (for small angles) amplitude.

At large angles, the motion becomes nonlinear: the period increases and the waveform is no longer purely sinusoidal. Try the "Large Angle" preset to see this effect.
Energy Conservation
In an ideal pendulum (no friction), total mechanical energy is conserved. At the highest point, all energy is gravitational PE. At the lowest point, all energy is kinetic.

KE = 1/2 m v2 where v = L * omega
PE = m g L (1 - cos theta)
