Quantum Coin Toss
Quantum Probability & Superposition · AP Physics Modern
Core Formulas

General quantum state is a superposition — α and β are probability amplitudes.

Born Rule: probability of measuring outcome |0⟩ or |1⟩.

Normalization: probabilities must sum to 1.

Concept Explanation

A classical coin has a definite face — it's either heads or tails before you look. A quantum coin is genuinely in both states simultaneously. The state |ψ⟩ = α|0⟩ + β|1⟩ is not ignorance — it is physical reality.

When you measure the quantum coin, the superposition collapses to a single outcome with probability |α|² for |0⟩ and |β|² for |1⟩. Unlike classical probability, there is no deeper hidden variable — the outcome is irreducibly random (Bell's theorem).

The Bloch sphere visualizes a qubit: |0⟩ is the north pole, |1⟩ is the south pole, and any superposition is a point on the surface. Angle θ sets the polar angle; φ sets the azimuthal phase.

Knowledge Cards
🔵 The Bloch sphere parametrization: |ψ⟩ = cos(θ/2)|0⟩ + e^(iφ)sin(θ/2)|1⟩. So |α|² = cos²(θ/2).
⚠️ Misconception: Measurement doesn't reveal a pre-existing value. It creates the outcome. (Proved by Bell experiments, 2022 Nobel Prize)
🔗 Large N limit: quantum statistics converge to classical binomial distribution — the Law of Large Numbers holds for quantum systems too.
Why It Matters
  • Quantum computers use qubits in superposition for exponential speedup on certain problems
  • Quantum cryptography uses measurement collapse to guarantee eavesdropping detection
  • Quantum random number generators produce truly random bits — impossible classically
