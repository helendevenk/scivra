Theory
Superposition: A quantum coin exists in a superposition of Heads and Tails simultaneously until measured. The probability of each outcome is determined by the squared amplitude of its coefficient in the quantum state. Unlike a classical coin flip (deterministic chaos), quantum randomness is fundamentally probabilistic — no hidden variables can predict the outcome.
