Kirchhoff's Laws

KCL: Sum of currents at any node = 0.

KVL: Sum of voltage drops around any loop = 0.

RC charging: voltage approaches EMF exponentially.

Voltage divider: output is fraction of input.

Concept

Kirchhoff's laws are the governing rules of every circuit. KCL follows from conservation of charge: charge can't accumulate at a node in steady state. KVL follows from conservation of energy: a charge carrier traveling any closed loop must return to the same potential.

For a capacitor in an RC circuit, the charging curve is exponential — the capacitor charges rapidly at first and slows as the voltage difference shrinks. Time constant τ = RC determines how fast this happens.

Wheatstone Bridge

When R1/R2 = R3/R4 the bridge is balanced: zero current through the galvanometer. Used to measure unknown resistances to high precision.

Node Voltage Method

Choose a reference (ground) node. Write KCL at each remaining node. Solve the linear system. This simulation does exactly that — live.
