Knowledge Check
1. Which nucleus has the highest binding energy per nucleon, making it the most stable?
A. Hydrogen-1 (1 proton)
B. Helium-4 (2p + 2n)
C. Iron-56 (26p + 30n)
D. Uranium-238 (92p + 146n)
Iron-56 sits at the peak of the binding energy curve with ~8.79 MeV per nucleon. Nuclei lighter than Fe-56 can release energy by fusion; nuclei heavier than Fe-56 can release energy by fission. This is why Fe-56 is the most tightly bound nucleus.
2. A carbon-12 nucleus has 6 protons and 6 neutrons. What is its mass number A?
A. 6
B. 12
C. 72
D. 36
The mass number A = Z + N, where Z is the number of protons and N is the number of neutrons. For carbon-12: A = 6 + 6 = 12. The mass number represents the total number of nucleons (protons + neutrons) in the nucleus.
