Core Formulas

Concept Explanation

The Lorentz force F = q(E + v × B) governs how charged particles move in electromagnetic fields. In a pure B field, the magnetic force always acts perpendicular to velocity — it does no work but continuously redirects the particle into circular (or helical) motion.

The cyclotron radius r = mv/(qB) shows that heavier particles orbit wider and slower particles orbit tighter. A velocity selector uses crossed E and B fields where qE = qvB, letting only particles with v = E/B pass through undeflected.

Historical Insight

H.A. Lorentz formulated this force law in 1895. The cyclotron, invented by Ernest Lawrence in 1930, exploits the mass-independent period to accelerate particles to high energies — still used in cancer treatment today.

Common Misconception

The magnetic force does NO work on a charged particle (F ⊥ v always), so it cannot change the particle's kinetic energy or speed — only its direction. Only the electric force can accelerate particles.
