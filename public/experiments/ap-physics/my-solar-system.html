Newton's Law of Gravitation
Every mass attracts every other mass. The force is proportional to the product of the two masses and inversely proportional to the square of the distance between their centers.
Newton's Second Law
The net gravitational force on each body determines its acceleration. With multiple bodies, forces from all other bodies are summed vectorially.

Q1: In a two-body Sun-Earth system, if the Earth's mass were doubled while keeping its orbit radius the same, what happens to the gravitational force on it?
Correct! F = Gm₁m₂/r². Doubling m₂ doubles the force, since the equation is linear in each mass.
Q2: The "Figure Eight" orbit is an example of which type of problem in classical mechanics?
Correct! The Figure Eight is a remarkable periodic solution to the three-body problem discovered by Moore (1993) and proven by Chenciner & Montgomery (2000). Three equal masses chase each other along a single figure-8 shaped curve.
