Concept Explanation

A generator converts mechanical rotation into electrical energy via Faraday's Law. When a coil rotates in a uniform magnetic field, the magnetic flux through it changes as Φ = NBA·cos(ωt). The induced EMF is ε = −dΦ/dt = NBAω·sin(ωt).

The output is naturally sinusoidal (AC) because the angle between the coil normal and the field changes continuously. Maximum EMF occurs when the coil plane is parallel to B (flux change is fastest), and zero EMF when the coil is perpendicular to B (flux is at its peak — no change).

Historical Insight

Faraday built the first practical generator (dynamo) in 1831. Today's power plants use the same principle — just with steam or water turbines instead of hand cranks, spinning at exactly 60 Hz (US) or 50 Hz (Europe).

Common Misconception

Maximum EMF does NOT occur when the coil is perpendicular to B. EMF peaks when the coil is parallel to B, because that's when flux is changing fastest (dΦ/dt is maximum).

Why It Matters

• All AC power plants: coal, nuclear, hydro, wind — all generators
• EV regenerative braking converts kinetic energy back to electricity
