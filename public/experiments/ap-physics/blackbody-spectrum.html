Ultraviolet Catastrophe
Classical physics (Rayleigh-Jeans law) predicts infinite energy at short wavelengths. Toggle the R-J curve to see how it diverges. Planck resolved this by proposing energy quantization: E = nhf. This was the birth of quantum mechanics.
Why Stars Have Colors
Hotter stars emit more at shorter wavelengths (Wien's law). A 3000 K red dwarf peaks in infrared, the Sun (5778 K) peaks in green-yellow, and a 10000 K star peaks in UV, appearing blue-white to our eyes.
