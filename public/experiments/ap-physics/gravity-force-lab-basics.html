Core Law
\(F = \frac{Gm_1 m_2}{r^2}\)
Newton's universal gravitation. G = 6.674×10⁻¹¹ N·m²/kg²
\(g = \frac{GM}{R^2}\)
Surface gravitational field strength. Earth: g = 9.8 m/s²
\(v_{orbit} = \sqrt{\frac{GM}{r}}\)
Circular orbital speed at radius r
\(v_{esc} = \sqrt{\frac{2GM}{R}}\)
Escape velocity from surface of mass M, radius R
Inverse Square Law

Double the distance → force becomes ¼ as strong. Triple → 1/9. This is the inverse-square relationship.

Gravity field lines spread over spherical shells: area = 4πr². Force per unit area ∝ 1/r².

\(T^2 = \frac{4\pi^2}{GM} r^3\)
Kepler's 3rd Law: orbital period T vs radius r

Tidal Forces
The near side of Earth feels stronger gravity from the Moon than the far side. This differential force stretches Earth, creating ocean tides.
Weight vs Mass

Mass (kg) = invariant property. Weight W = mg depends on local g. On Moon: g = 1.62 m/s², so you weigh 1/6 of Earth weight.
