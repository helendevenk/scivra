Force vs Distance
Quick Quiz
1. If the distance between two masses is tripled, the gravitational force:
A) Becomes one-third
B) Triples
C) Becomes one-ninth
D) Stays the same
Correct! F ∝ 1/r², so tripling r reduces F by a factor of 3² = 9. This is the inverse-square law applied to gravity.
2. If both masses are doubled, the gravitational force:
A) Doubles
B) Quadruples
C) Stays the same
D) Increases eight-fold
Correct! F ∝ m₁m₂. Doubling both masses: F' = G(2m₁)(2m₂)/r² = 4 × Gm₁m₂/r² = 4F.
