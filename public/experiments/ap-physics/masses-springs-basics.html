What is Hooke's Law?

When a spring is stretched or compressed from its natural length, it pushes back with a force proportional to how far it was displaced. The proportionality constant k is the spring constant, measured in N/m.

A larger k means a stiffer spring that resists deformation more strongly. A smaller k means a softer, more flexible spring.

Period and Mass

The period T is the time for one complete oscillation. It depends on mass and spring constant, but not on amplitude (for ideal springs).

Heavier masses oscillate more slowly (longer period). Stiffer springs oscillate faster (shorter period).
