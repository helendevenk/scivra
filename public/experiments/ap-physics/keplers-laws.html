1st Law: Elliptical Orbits
Every planet moves in an elliptical orbit with the star at one focus. The semi-latus rectum l = a(1 - e²) defines the shape.
2nd Law: Equal Areas
A line from the star to the planet sweeps out equal areas in equal time intervals. The planet moves faster near perihelion and slower near aphelion.
3rd Law: Harmonic Law
The square of the orbital period is proportional to the cube of the semi-major axis. This ratio is the same for all orbits around the same star.

Quiz
Q1: A planet is at aphelion (farthest point). How does its speed compare to when it is at perihelion?
Correct: C. By Kepler's 2nd Law (equal areas), the planet must move slower at aphelion (farther from the star) to sweep the same area per unit time as at perihelion (closer, faster).
Q2: Planet X has a semi-major axis 4 times that of Planet Y, orbiting the same star. How do their periods compare?
Correct: B. By Kepler's 3rd Law, T² ∝ a³. If a_X = 4a_Y, then T_X² = 4³ T_Y² = 64 T_Y², so T_X = 8 T_Y.
