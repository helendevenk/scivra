Core Formulas
Voltage and current with phase shift φ. For purely resistive load, φ = 0.
Total impedance from R, inductive reactance X_L, and capacitive reactance X_C.
Resonance when X_L = X_C → minimum impedance, maximum current.
Concept

In AC circuits, resistors, inductors, and capacitors respond differently to alternating current. A resistor dissipates power (in phase). An inductor stores energy in a magnetic field and causes current to lag voltage by 90°. A capacitor stores energy in an electric field and causes current to lead voltage by 90°.

Impedance Z is the AC analog of resistance. At resonance (f₀ = 1/(2π√LC)), X_L = X_C cancels and Z = R — the circuit draws maximum current. This principle underlies radio tuners, filters, and wireless charging.

Historical Fact
Nikola Tesla's AC system beat Edison's DC in the "War of Currents" (1880s–1890s) because AC transformers allow long-distance transmission at high voltage with low current, minimizing I²R losses.
Common Misconception
Capacitors and inductors consume no real power (P_avg = 0 for pure reactive elements). They store and release energy each cycle — reactive power Q describes this exchange but does no net work.
Why It Matters (AP Physics)
AP Physics 2 covers RLC circuits, resonance, and phasors. Understanding phase relationships is essential for power engineering, radio frequency design, and medical imaging (MRI).
