Concept Explanation

Rutherford's 1911 scattering experiment revealed the nuclear model: a dense positive nucleus surrounded by electrons. But this model predicted electrons would spiral inward in nanoseconds — it was unstable.

Bohr (1913) fixed this with a radical postulate: electrons only exist in quantized orbits with angular momentum L = nℏ. In these orbits, electrons do not radiate. Transitions between orbits emit or absorb photons with exactly ΔE = hf.

The modern quantum model replaces circular orbits with probability clouds (orbitals), but Bohr's energy formula E_n = -13.6/n² eV remains exactly correct for hydrogen.

Spectral Series

Lyman Series (→ n=1): UV photons. E ≈ 10-13.6 eV. Discovered 1906.
Balmer Series (→ n=2): Visible + near-UV. H-alpha (656nm, red), H-beta (486nm, blue). Discovered 1885.
Paschen Series (→ n=3): Infrared. First observed 1908. Explains near-IR stellar spectra.
