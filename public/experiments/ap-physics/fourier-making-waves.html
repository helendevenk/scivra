Concept
Joseph Fourier proved that any periodic function can be decomposed into — or built from — a sum of simple sine and cosine waves. This is the Fourier series.
Each harmonic contributes a specific frequency (n·f₁) and amplitude. The frequency spectrum shows how much of each harmonic is present. Sharp edges in a waveform (like a square wave) require many high-frequency harmonics to represent.
History: Fourier published his theorem in 1822. It revolutionized physics, engineering, and later enabled JPEG compression and MP3 audio.
Insight: The more harmonics you add to a square wave, the closer you get — but Gibbs phenomenon causes ~9% overshoot near discontinuities, regardless of how many terms.
AP Link: Understanding harmonics directly connects to standing waves, musical acoustics, and wave superposition.
