Hooke's Law

When a spring is displaced from its equilibrium position, it exerts a restoring force proportional to the displacement. The constant k (spring constant) measures the stiffness.

A mass hanging from a vertical spring settles at equilibrium where mg = kx_eq. Displace it further and it oscillates in simple harmonic motion.

With damping (friction or air resistance), the amplitude decays exponentially: A(t) = A_0 e^(-bt/2m). The system still oscillates but gradually comes to rest.
