Atomic Interactions Lennard-Jones Potential

Core Formulas

Pair potential: repulsive core (r⁻¹²) + attractive well (r⁻⁶)

Interatomic force derived as F = −dU/dr

Equilibrium separation where U is minimized, F = 0

Concept

Two atoms interact through a balance of forces: at short range, electron cloud overlap creates a strongly repulsive core (r⁻¹²). At medium range, induced-dipole attraction (van der Waals / r⁻⁶) pulls them together. The competition creates a potential energy well at req.

The parameter ε (epsilon) is the depth of the well — essentially the bond energy. σ (sigma) sets the effective atomic size. Noble gases like argon are well described by LJ with ε ≈ 0.01 eV and σ ≈ 0.34 nm.

When atoms approach from far away, they accelerate into the well, converting PE to KE. They oscillate around req unless energy is dissipated — the heart of molecular bonding.

🔬 Fun Fact
The r⁻¹² repulsion is purely empirical — chosen for computational convenience, not physical derivation. The r⁻⁶ attraction has real physical origin: London dispersion forces.
⚠️ Common Misconception
Atoms don't "touch" at r_eq. They settle where forces balance — still separated by their electron clouds.
🔗 AP Connection
LJ is the prototype for all bonding PE diagrams in AP Physics/Chemistry: well depth = dissociation energy, curvature = bond stiffness.

Why It Matters

  • • Molecular dynamics simulations use LJ to model gases, liquids, and solids
  • • Explains why noble gases condense at low temperatures
