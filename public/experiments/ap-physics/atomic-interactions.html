Knowledge Check
1. At what separation does the Lennard-Jones potential reach its minimum (equilibrium)?
A. r = σ
B. r = 21/6σ ≈ 1.122σ
C. r = 2σ
D. r = σ/2
The equilibrium distance is found by setting F = -dV/dr = 0. Solving gives rmin = 21/6σ ≈ 1.122σ, where the potential energy equals -ε (the well depth).
2. Which term in the Lennard-Jones potential dominates at very short distances (r « σ)?
A. The (σ/r)12 repulsive term
B. The (σ/r)6 attractive term
C. Both terms are equal
D. Neither — the potential is zero
At very short distances, r-12 grows much faster than r-6. The repulsive (σ/r)12 term models Pauli repulsion from overlapping electron clouds and completely dominates, causing V(r) to rise steeply toward +∞.
