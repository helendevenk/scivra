Key Formulas

Mechanical Advantage: $MA = \frac{F_{out}}{F_{in}}$

where $F_{out}$ is output force, $F_{in}$ is input force

Work: $W = F \times d$

where $W$ is work, $F$ is force, $d$ is distance

⚙️ What are Simple Machines?

Simple machines are basic devices that make work easier by changing the direction or magnitude of a force. They are the building blocks of all complex machines.

🔧 Lever

A lever uses a fulcrum (pivot point) to multiply force. Examples: seesaw, crowbar, scissors. The farther from the fulcrum you push, the more force you get!

🪢 Pulley

A pulley uses a wheel and rope to lift heavy objects. Multiple pulleys can multiply your force, making it easier to lift heavy loads.

📐 Inclined Plane

A ramp that makes it easier to move objects to a higher position. You use less force but move the object over a longer distance. Examples: ramps, slides, stairs.

🎡 Wheel and Axle

A wheel attached to a rod (axle). Turning the wheel creates more force at the axle. Examples: doorknobs, steering wheels, screwdrivers.
