Key Formulas

How Phases Work

The Moon does not glow on its own. It reflects light from the Sun. As the Moon travels around Earth, we see different portions of its sunlit half — these are the phases.

The full orbit takes about 29.5 days — this is called a synodic month or lunar cycle.

The 8 Phases

🌑 New Moon — Moon is between Earth and Sun. We see the dark side.
🌒 Waxing Crescent — A thin sliver grows on the right side.
🌓 First Quarter — The right half is illuminated.
🌔 Waxing Gibbous — More than half lit and growing.
🌕 Full Moon — Entire face lit; opposite the Sun.
🌖 Waning Gibbous — More than half, now shrinking.
🌗 Last Quarter — Left half illuminated.
🌘 Waning Crescent — Thin sliver fades on the left.

Why It Matters

Ancient farmers planted crops using Moon cycles. Ocean tides rise and fall with the Moon's gravity. Early calendars were built around the lunar cycle!

Eclipses happen when the Moon lines up precisely with Earth and Sun during New or Full Moon phases.
