Learning Objectives

●Classify stars by color, size, and temperature
●Explain what a star is and how it produces energy
●Understand the stellar lifecycle from nebula to remnant
●Identify familiar constellations and our solar system's place

Key Formulas

Star Types by Color

🔵 Blue/White Stars — Hottest (10,000–50,000 K). Burn bright but die young. (e.g., Rigel)
⚪ White Stars — Very hot (~7,000 K). (e.g., Sirius)
🌕 Yellow Stars — Medium temperature (~6,000 K). Our Sun is a yellow dwarf!
🟠 Orange Stars — Cooler (~4,000 K). (e.g., Arcturus)
🔴 Red Stars — Coolest (~3,000 K). Can be red dwarfs (tiny, long-lived) or red giants (dying).

Stellar Lifecycle

Nebula → Protostar → Main Sequence Star → Red Giant/Supergiant → White Dwarf / Neutron Star / Black Hole

Our Sun is about halfway through its 10-billion-year life.

Fun Facts

The nearest star beyond our Sun is Proxima Centauri, ~4.24 light-years away. Light from it takes over 4 years to reach us!

There are more stars in the universe than grains of sand on all Earth's beaches combined.

Why It Matters

Stars forge the chemical elements inside them through nuclear fusion. Every atom of carbon, oxygen, and iron in your body was made in a star!
