Key Formulas

Key Vocabulary

💧 Solvent — The liquid that does the dissolving (usually water).
🟡 Solute — The substance being dissolved (like salt or sugar).
🧂 Solution — A homogeneous mixture where solute is evenly spread throughout.
📊 Concentration — How much solute is dissolved in the solvent.

Solution Types

Dilute: Very little solute — particles spread far apart, pale color.

Saturated: Maximum amount dissolved — any more won't dissolve.

Supersaturated: More dissolved than normally possible — unstable, crystals form.

Separating Mixtures

Filtration removes undissolved solids. Evaporation recovers dissolved solute. Distillation separates liquids with different boiling points.

Why It Matters

Solutions are everywhere — blood, seawater, soft drinks, medicines. Understanding concentration is essential for chemistry, cooking, and medicine dosing.
