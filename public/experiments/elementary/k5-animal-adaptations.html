Adaptation Formula

Three Habitats

🧊 Arctic (Fox) — White fur for camouflage in snow. Thick fat layer for insulation. Small ears reduce heat loss. Wide paws for walking on snow.
🏜️ Desert (Camel) — Hump stores fat (energy). Thick eyelashes keep out sand. Wide feet distribute weight on sand. Can survive days without water.
🌴 Rainforest (Toucan) — Large colorful beak for reaching fruit. Bright colors for mate attraction. Zygodactyl feet grip branches. Great balance for canopy life.

Types of Adaptations

Structural: Physical features — body shape, fur, feathers, claws, beak shape.

Behavioral: Actions — migration, hibernation, hunting patterns, social behaviors.

Why It Matters

Adaptations evolve over thousands of generations through natural selection. Animals with better adaptations survive and reproduce, passing traits to offspring.
