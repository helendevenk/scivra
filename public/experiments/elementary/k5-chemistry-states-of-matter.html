Key Formulas

Kinetic Energy: $KE = \frac{3}{2}k_BT$

where $k_B$ is Boltzmann constant, $T$ is temperature

Ideal Gas Law: $PV = nRT$

where $P$ is pressure, $V$ is volume, $n$ is moles, $R$ is gas constant

🧊 Solid State

Particles are tightly packed in a fixed arrangement. They vibrate in place but don't move freely. Solids have definite shape and volume.

💧 Liquid State

Particles are close together but can slide past each other. Liquids have definite volume but take the shape of their container.

💨 Gas State

Particles are far apart and move freely at high speeds. Gases have no definite shape or volume and expand to fill their container.

🔄 Phase Transitions

Matter changes state when energy is added or removed. Melting, freezing, evaporation, and condensation are examples of phase transitions.

🌡️ Temperature & Energy

Temperature measures the average kinetic energy of particles. Higher temperature means faster particle motion and more energy.
