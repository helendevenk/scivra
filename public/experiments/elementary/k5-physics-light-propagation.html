How Light Travels
Straight Lines!
Light always travels in straight lines (called rays). That's why shadows have sharp edges — light can't bend around objects. Speed of light: 300,000,000 m/s (fastest thing in the universe!).
c = 3 × 10⁸ m/s
Speed of light in a vacuum (nothing travels faster)
Reflection
Angle In = Angle Out
When light hits a mirror, the angle of reflection equals the angle of incidence. Both angles are measured from the "normal" (perpendicular to the mirror surface). A plane mirror creates a virtual, upright, same-size image!
θ_incidence = θ_reflection
The law of reflection — always measured from the normal
Refraction
Light Bends in New Materials
When light moves from air into glass or water, it slows down and bends. This is refraction. It's why a spoon in a glass of water looks bent or broken at the surface — the light changes direction!
Rainbow Science
A prism or raindrop splits white light into a spectrum (ROYGBIV: Red, Orange, Yellow, Green, Blue, Indigo, Violet). Different colors bend by different amounts — red bends least, violet bends most.
Shadows
No Light = Shadow
A shadow forms where light is blocked by an opaque object. The shadow is on the opposite side from the light source. Bigger object, bigger shadow. Closer to light source, bigger shadow.
