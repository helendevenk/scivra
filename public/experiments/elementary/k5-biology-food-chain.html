Key Formulas

Energy Transfer: $E_{next} = E_{current} \times \eta$

where $\eta$ is efficiency (typically 10%)

10% Rule: Only about 10% of energy transfers to the next trophic level

90% is lost as heat, movement, and life processes

🌱 Producers

Plants and algae that make their own food using sunlight through photosynthesis. They are the foundation of every food chain.

🐰 Primary Consumers

Herbivores that eat plants. Examples: rabbits, deer, caterpillars. They get energy by eating producers.

🦊 Secondary Consumers

Carnivores that eat herbivores. Examples: foxes, snakes, frogs. They are predators in the food chain.

⚡ Energy Flow

Energy flows from the sun to producers, then to consumers. Each level gets only about 10% of the energy from the level below.

⚖️ Balance

Ecosystems need balance. Too many predators can wipe out prey. Too few predators can lead to overpopulation of prey.
