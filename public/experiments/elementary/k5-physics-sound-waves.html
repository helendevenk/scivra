What is Sound?
Sound is Vibration!
Sound is made by vibrating objects. When a guitar string vibrates, it pushes air molecules back and forth, creating a wave that travels to your ears. No vibration = no sound!
Sound Needs a Medium
Sound travels through solids, liquids, and gases. It CANNOT travel through empty space (vacuum). That's why there's no sound in outer space! Speed in air ≈ 343 m/s.
Pitch & Frequency
High Frequency = High Pitch
Frequency = how many vibrations per second (Hz). High Hz = squeaky/high pitch. Low Hz = deep/low pitch. A dog whistle is ~40,000 Hz — too high for humans to hear!
Musical note A = 440 Hz
Middle A on a piano vibrates 440 times every second
Human Hearing Range
Humans hear 20 Hz to 20,000 Hz. Bats and dolphins use echolocation with ultrasound (>20,000 Hz) to "see" in the dark or underwater.
Loudness & Amplitude
Big Vibration = Loud Sound
Amplitude = size of the vibration. Big amplitude = loud (high decibels). Small amplitude = quiet. A whisper is ~30 dB. A jet engine is ~140 dB — that's 10,000× louder!
Speed of sound = 343 m/s
In air at 20°C. Much faster in water (1500 m/s) and steel (5000 m/s)!
Speed in Different Materials
Solids > Liquids > Gases
Molecules are closer together in solids, so vibrations pass faster. Steel: ~5000 m/s. Water: ~1500 m/s. Air: ~343 m/s. That's why you can hear a train coming by putting your ear on the rail!
