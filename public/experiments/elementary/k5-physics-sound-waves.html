Key Formulas

Wave Speed: $v = f \lambda$

where $v$ is speed, $f$ is frequency, $\lambda$ is wavelength

Period: $T = \frac{1}{f}$

where $T$ is period (time for one cycle), $f$ is frequency

Doppler Effect: $f' = f \times \frac{v}{v \pm v_s}$

$f'$ = observed frequency, $v$ = sound speed, $v_s$ = source speed. Use $-$ when approaching, $+$ when receding.

What is Sound?

Sound is a vibration that travels through air (or other materials) as a wave. When objects vibrate, they create pressure waves that our ears detect as sound.

Frequency and Pitch

Frequency is how many waves pass a point per second (measured in Hz). Higher frequency means higher pitch. A dog whistle has very high frequency!

Amplitude and Volume

Amplitude is the height of the wave. Larger amplitude means louder sound. When you turn up the volume, you're increasing the amplitude of the sound waves.

Speed of Sound

Sound travels at about 343 m/s in air. In water it's about 1480 m/s, and in steel about 5960 m/s! Particles closer together transmit sound faster.

The Doppler Effect

When a sound source moves toward you, waves get compressed and the pitch sounds higher. When it moves away, waves stretch out and the pitch drops. That's why a siren changes pitch as it passes!

Musical Notes

Different musical notes have different frequencies. Middle A is 440 Hz. When you play an instrument, you're creating sound waves at specific frequencies.
