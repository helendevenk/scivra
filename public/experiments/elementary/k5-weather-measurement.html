K5 Earth Science — Grades 2-5 | Ages 7-11

Learning Objectives

●Name the four main weather measuring instruments
●Explain what each instrument measures

●Understand units: °C, hPa, m/s, mm

Key Formulas

The 4 Instruments

🌡️ Thermometer — Measures air temperature in °C or °F. Uses expanding liquid or electronic sensors.
📊 Barometer — Measures atmospheric pressure in hPa (hectopascals) or millibars. High = clear weather, Low = storms coming.
💨 Anemometer — Measures wind speed in m/s or km/h. Cup-style spins faster in stronger winds.
🌧️ Rain Gauge — Collects and measures precipitation in millimeters. A cylinder open to the sky.

Reading Weather Data

Rising pressure = improving weather. Falling pressure = deteriorating weather. Meteorologists combine all four readings to forecast conditions.

Why It Matters

Accurate weather measurement enables forecasts that protect lives, guide aviation, farming, and daily planning. The global weather network has thousands of stations!
