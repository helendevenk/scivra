Key Formulas

The Four Stages

☀️ Evaporation — Heat from the Sun turns liquid water into water vapor that rises into the air.
☁️ Condensation — Water vapor cools high in the sky and turns back into tiny droplets, forming clouds.
🌧️ Precipitation — Water falls back to Earth as rain, snow, sleet, or hail.
🏞️ Collection/Runoff — Water collects in oceans, lakes, rivers, and underground, starting the cycle again.

Fun Facts

The same water molecules have been cycling through Earth's system for billions of years. The water you drink might have been in a dinosaur!

About 97% of Earth's water is in the oceans. Only 3% is fresh water, and most of that is frozen in glaciers.

Why It Matters

The water cycle is essential for life. It distributes fresh water around the planet, regulates temperature, and shapes landscapes through erosion and deposition.
