Core Formulas

Kinetic Energy

Energy of motion. Greater mass and speed mean more kinetic energy.

Gravitational Potential Energy

Energy stored due to height. Higher position means more potential energy.

Conservation of Energy

Energy cannot be created or destroyed, only transformed from one form to another.

Electrical Power

Power equals voltage times current. Determines energy consumption rate.

Kinetic Energy

Energy of motion. A rolling ball, a moving car, or flowing wind all possess kinetic energy proportional to mass and velocity squared.

Potential Energy

Stored energy due to position or configuration. A ball at the top of a hill has gravitational PE that converts to KE as it rolls down.

Thermal Energy

Internal energy from molecular motion. Friction converts mechanical energy to heat. Incandescent bulbs waste ~90% of energy as heat.

Electrical Energy

Energy carried by moving electrons. Wind turbines convert kinetic energy of air into electrical energy through electromagnetic induction.

Light Energy

Electromagnetic radiation visible to our eyes. LEDs convert electrical energy to light far more efficiently than incandescent bulbs.

Conservation Law

Total energy in a closed system remains constant. Energy transforms between forms but the total never changes. Losses are always accounted for.
