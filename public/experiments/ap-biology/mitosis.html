Phase Overview
G1 — Cell grows, synthesizes proteins, organelles. Checkpoint: is cell large enough? DNA intact?
S phase — DNA replication. Each chromosome duplicated → sister chromatids joined at centromere.
G2 — Cell continues growing, preps mitotic spindle proteins. Checkpoint: DNA fully replicated?
Prophase — Chromatin condenses into visible chromosomes. Spindle forms. Nuclear envelope breaks down.
Metaphase — Chromosomes align at metaphase plate. Spindle checkpoint: all kinetochores attached?
Anaphase — Sister chromatids separate. Motor proteins walk chromosomes to poles.
Telophase — Nuclear envelopes reform. Chromosomes decondense. Cell elongates.
Cytokinesis — Cytoplasm divides. Cleavage furrow (animals) or cell plate (plants).
Why It Matters
  • Uncontrolled mitosis = cancer (cell cycle checkpoints fail)
  • Colchicine blocks tubulin polymerization → arrests cells at metaphase (used in cancer therapy)
  • MPF (Maturation Promoting Factor = CDK1 + Cyclin B) drives G2→M transition
  • Aneuploidy from spindle checkpoint failure → trisomy 21 (Down syndrome)
