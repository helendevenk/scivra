Succession Stages

Stage 1: Pioneer (0–15 yr)

Lichens, mosses, bare rock microbes. Soil formation begins.

Stage 2: Annual Plants (15–40 yr)

Grasses and fast-growing annuals establish thin soil layer.

Stage 3: Shrubs (40–100 yr)

Woody shrubs dominate, creating shade and deeper root systems.

Stage 4: Young Forest (100–250 yr)

Pioneer trees (birch, aspen) colonize. Diversity peaks.

Stage 5: Climax Forest (250+ yr)

Stable old-growth community. Shade-tolerant trees dominate.

Primary vs Secondary

Primary: starts on bare substrate (no soil). Secondary: disturbed area with existing soil — much faster recovery.
