Overall free energy change. Negative ΔG means spontaneous exergonic reaction.

Pathway Overview

Cellular respiration extracts chemical energy from glucose in three coordinated stages. Glycolysis in the cytoplasm splits glucose into two pyruvate molecules, yielding a net 2 ATP and 2 NADH. Pyruvate oxidation in the mitochondrial matrix converts pyruvate to acetyl-CoA + CO₂ + NADH. The Krebs cycle (2 turns per glucose) produces 2 ATP, 6 NADH, and 2 FADH₂. The electron transport chain uses these electron carriers to pump H⁺ ions, driving ATP synthase to produce 32–34 ATP.

Why It Matters — AP Bio
Big Idea 2 (Energy). AP Exam free-response frequently asks students to compare aerobic vs. anaerobic ATP yield, explain how inhibitors (cyanide, oligomycin) affect ETC, and describe how O₂ availability regulates pathway selection. Understanding compartmentalization (cytoplasm vs. matrix vs. inner membrane) is essential.
Common Misconception
The "38 ATP" textbook value is theoretical maximum. Actual yield is 30–32 ATP due to proton leak and membrane transport costs. NADH from glycolysis enters via malate-aspartate or glycerol-3-phosphate shuttles, affecting the count.
