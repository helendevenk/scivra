Membrane Transport Diffusion · Osmosis · Active Transport · AP Biology
Core Formulas
Fick's Law: net diffusion rate is proportional to concentration gradient and membrane permeability.
Osmotic pressure determines water movement across semi-permeable membranes.
Na⁺/K⁺-ATPase pumps 3 Na⁺ out and 2 K⁺ in per ATP — the engine of cell potential.
Concept

The phospholipid bilayer is selectively permeable. Small nonpolar molecules (O₂, CO₂) pass freely. Ions and large polar molecules require protein channels or carriers. Passive transport moves substances down concentration gradients (no energy). Active transport moves them against gradients using ATP.

The Na⁺/K⁺ pump maintains the resting membrane potential (−70 mV in neurons) by continuously pumping 3 Na⁺ out and 2 K⁺ in per ATP hydrolysis cycle.

Transport Types
Simple diffusion (lipid soluble) · Facilitated diffusion (channel/carrier) · Primary active transport (ATP) · Secondary active (cotransport) · Osmosis (water via aquaporins)
Common Misconception
Osmosis is not "water moving toward solutes" — water moves down its own concentration gradient (from high water potential to low water potential).
Why It Matters (AP Bio)
Membrane transport underlies nerve impulses, kidney filtration, plant water uptake, and drug delivery. It's a foundational concept tested in FRQ and MCQ every year.
