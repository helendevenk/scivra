Evidence of Evolution Four Lines of Evidence · AP Biology
Four Lines of Evidence
① Fossil Record: Temporal succession of life forms. Index fossils date rock strata. Transitional fossils (e.g., Tiktaalik, Archaeopteryx) show intermediate forms.
② Comparative Anatomy: Homologous structures (same origin, different function) in human, whale, bat, and horse forelimbs reveal common ancestry.
③ Molecular Phylogeny: Cytochrome c amino acid sequences: human/chimp differ by 0 aa, human/dog by 13, human/yeast by 51. More similarity = more recent common ancestor.
④ Biogeography: Island species most similar to nearby mainland species, not to geographically distant islands with similar climate (Darwin's finches, Galápagos).
Concept

Evolution by natural selection (Darwin, 1859) is supported by convergent independent lines of evidence — fossil record, anatomy, molecular biology, and biogeography. No single line is definitive; their convergence makes evolution one of the most well-supported theories in science.

Molecular evidence is the most precise: DNA/protein sequences directly reflect genetic history. Phylogenetic trees built from cytochrome c are nearly identical to those built from morphology — despite the methods being completely independent.

Fossil Record Key Facts
• Life on Earth: ~3.8 billion years ago (prokaryotes)
• Eukaryotes: ~2.1 bya
• Multicellular life: ~600 mya (Ediacaran)
• Cambrian explosion: ~541 mya (most animal phyla)
• Mass extinctions: ~5 major events
• Homo sapiens: ~300,000 years ago
Common Misconception
"Humans evolved from chimpanzees" is wrong. Humans and chimps share a common ancestor (diverged ~6-7 mya). Evolution is a branching process, not a linear ladder — modern chimps are not our ancestors, they are our evolutionary cousins.
Why It Matters
Understanding evolution predicts antibiotic resistance (bacteria evolve), cancer progression (natural selection on tumor cells), and pandemic emergence (viral evolution). Evolution is the unifying theory of all biology.
