Core Equations
Overall photosynthesis: light energy converts CO₂ + H₂O into glucose + O₂.
Quantum yield: moles of CO₂ fixed per mole of photons absorbed. Maximum theoretical ~0.125.
Light compensation point: photosynthesis rate equals respiration rate.
Z-Scheme Overview

Light reactions occur in the thylakoid membrane. PSII absorbs photons (680 nm peak), exciting electrons that pass to the plastoquinone pool (PQ). Water splitting (2 H₂O → 4 H⁺ + 4e⁻ + O₂) replenishes electrons. Electrons flow through cytochrome b6f complex → plastocyanin (PC) → PSI (700 nm peak) → ferredoxin (Fd) → NADP⁺ reductase → NADPH. The Z-shaped energy profile traces electron energy vs. redox potential.
