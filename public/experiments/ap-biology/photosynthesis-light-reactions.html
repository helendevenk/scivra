Photosynthesis: Light Reactions Thylakoid Membrane & Z-Scheme

Core Equations

Overall light reaction: 12 photons split 6 water molecules

ATP synthase couples proton gradient to ATP synthesis

Z-scheme: two photosystems in series to reach NADP⁺ reduction potential

Concept

Light reactions occur on the thylakoid membrane. PSII absorbs photons at ~680 nm, using energy to split water (photolysis), releasing O₂ and electrons. These electrons travel through the electron transport chain (plastoquinone → Cyt b6f → plastocyanin) to PSI, which absorbs at ~700 nm and further energizes electrons to reduce NADP⁺ to NADPH. The proton gradient drives ATP synthase. Together NADPH and ATP power the Calvin cycle.

🔬 Z-Scheme
Called the Z-scheme because the energy diagram traces a Z-shaped path: low energy at PSII, mid at plastoquinone, boosted at PSI, high at ferredoxin/NADPH.
⚠️ Far-Red Effect
Light above 700 nm (far-red) alone is inefficient. The Emerson enhancement effect shows both photosystems must be active together for maximum efficiency.
