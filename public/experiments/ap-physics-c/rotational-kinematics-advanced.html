AP Physics C: Mechanics

Core Equations

Moment of Inertia

I = Σmᵢrᵢ² quantifies resistance to rotational acceleration. The distribution of mass relative to the axis determines I. Objects with mass concentrated at the rim have larger I than those with mass near the center.

Rolling Without Slipping

The contact point has zero velocity: v_cm = Rω and a_cm = Rα. On an incline, the translational and rotational equations combine: a = g sinθ / (1 + I/MR²).

Parallel Axis Theorem

I about any parallel axis = I_cm + Md². Lets you find I about any axis if you know I about the center of mass axis.

Why It Matters (AP Physics C)

Rolling dynamics, parallel axis theorem, and I calculations are heavily tested. The hollow cylinder always reaches the bottom last because more energy is stored in rotation. Understanding this is essential for both MC and FR sections.
