Core Equations

Impedance & Phase

Impedance Z = √(R² + (X_L − X_C)²) where X_L = ωL and X_C = 1/ωC. The phase angle φ = arctan((X_L − X_C)/R) tells you whether the circuit is inductive (φ>0) or capacitive (φ<0).

Resonance

At resonance ω₀ = 1/√(LC): X_L = X_C, Z = R (minimum), current is maximum, and VR = V_source. The Q-factor = ω₀L/R measures sharpness: higher Q → narrower bandwidth.

Phasor Diagram

Phasors rotate at ω rad/s. V_R is in phase with I, V_L leads I by 90°, V_C lags I by 90°. The total voltage V_total is the phasor sum. At resonance, V_L and V_C cancel exactly.

Why It Matters (AP Physics C: E&M)

RLC circuits are the culminating topic of AP Physics C E&M. You must be able to calculate Z, I_max, φ, ω₀, and Q-factor. The phasor method and resonance are core FR topics. Real-world: radio tuning, filter design, wireless charging.
