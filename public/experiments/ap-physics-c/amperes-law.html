Core Equations

Ampere's Law

The line integral of B around a closed Amperian loop equals μ₀ times the total current enclosed. Choose the loop symmetry to match the field geometry — circular for a wire, rectangular for a solenoid.

Straight Wire

For an infinite straight wire, B forms circles concentric with the wire. At radius r: B = μ₀I / 2πr. The field falls off as 1/r.

Solenoid

Inside an ideal solenoid with n turns/m: B = μ₀nI. The field is uniform and parallel to the axis. Outside: B ≈ 0. A rectangular Amperian loop shows only the interior leg contributes.

Toroid

For a toroid with N total turns and mean radius r: B = μ₀NI / 2πr inside. Outside: B = 0. The field is confined within the donut shape.

Why It Matters (AP Physics C: E&M)

Ampere's Law is one of Maxwell's four equations. It appears in every AP Physics C E&M exam. Mastery requires selecting the right Amperian loop and correctly identifying I_enclosed for non-uniform current distributions.
