Core Equations

Gauss's Law

The total electric flux through any closed surface equals the net charge enclosed divided by ε₀. Choose a Gaussian surface that matches the symmetry — spherical for point charges, cylindrical for line charges, pillbox for planes.

Point Charge

Gaussian sphere of radius r: E · 4πr² = Q/ε₀ → E = kQ/r². Field lines emanate radially. Flux is independent of sphere radius.

Infinite Line & Plane

Line charge (λ): Gaussian cylinder → E = λ/2πε₀r. Infinite plane (σ): pillbox → E = σ/2ε₀ (uniform, independent of distance).

Why It Matters (AP Physics C: E&M)

Gauss's Law is Maxwell Equation #1. AP Physics C E&M free response frequently asks you to derive E fields using Gauss's Law for all three geometries. The key skill is identifying and applying the right Gaussian surface.
