Concept

A coffee-cup calorimeter measures heat exchange at constant pressure (qₚ = ΔH). Heat flows from the warmer to the cooler component until thermal equilibrium is reached. The temperature change of the solution reveals the heat of reaction.

Exothermic reactions (ΔH < 0) raise the solution temperature. Endothermic reactions (ΔH > 0) lower it. The calorimeter is assumed to have negligible heat capacity.

AP Exam Tip
q_solution = mcΔT where ΔT = T_final - T_initial. q_rxn = -q_solution. Convert to molar enthalpy using moles of limiting reagent from the balanced equation.
Why It Matters
Calorimetry underpins food calorie labeling, industrial process heat management, and pharmaceuticals. Bomb calorimeters measure combustion ΔH at constant volume (qᵥ = ΔU).
