Tidal Bulge Mechanics

The Moon's gravity pulls ocean water toward it, creating a bulge on the near side. A second bulge forms on the far side due to the centrifugal effect of Earth-Moon orbit — the far side is pulled least by the Moon relative to Earth's center. This gives two high tides per day (~12.4 hr cycle). The Moon's gravity is ~2.2x stronger than the Sun's tidal effect due to distance.

Spring vs. Neap Tides

Spring Tides — Sun, Earth, Moon aligned (new/full Moon); gravitational forces add; extreme high and low tides
Neap Tides — Sun, Earth, Moon at 90° (quarter Moon); forces partially cancel; moderate tides
Perigean Spring Tides — Moon at perigee + spring tide; most extreme flooding risk
