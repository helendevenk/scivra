Scale Context

If Earth were a marble (1.3 cm), the Sun would be a 1.4 m ball 150 m away. Neptune would be 4.5 km distant. Proxima Centauri, our nearest stellar neighbor, would be 40,000 km away — farther than Earth's circumference!

The Kuiper Belt (30–50 AU) contains icy bodies: Pluto, Eris, Makemake. The Oort Cloud (~50,000 AU) is a hypothesized spherical shell of comets — the outer boundary of the solar system.

Voyager 1, launched 1977, crossed the heliopause (~123 AU) in 2012 and is now in interstellar space.

Knowledge Cards
Asteroid Belt (2.2–3.2 AU)
Between Mars and Jupiter. Contains ~1 million asteroids >1 km. Largest: Ceres (940 km, now a dwarf planet). Total mass ≈ 4% of Moon's mass — too spread out to form a planet (Jupiter's gravity disrupts it).
Saturn's Rings
Extend 280,000 km from Saturn but only ~10 m thick on average. Composed of 93% water ice + silicate dust. Origin: likely a destroyed moon or captured comet ~100 Ma ago.
Jupiter's Great Red Spot
A persistent anticyclonic storm larger than Earth. Observed since ~1665. Shrinking since the 19th century — currently ~16,000 km wide. May disappear within decades.
Why It Matters

Understanding solar system scale is foundational for space exploration mission planning, planetary protection (asteroid deflection), understanding habitability zones around other stars (exoplanet science), and calibrating our place in the cosmos — the basis of scientific humility and curiosity.
