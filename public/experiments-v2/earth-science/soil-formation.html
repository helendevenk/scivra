Soil Horizons (Soil Profile)

O Horizon — Organic: undecomposed litter and humus
A Horizon — Topsoil: dark, humus-rich, most fertile
E Horizon — Eluviation: leached layer, pale colored
B Horizon — Subsoil: accumulation of clay and minerals
C Horizon — Regolith: weathered parent material
R Horizon — Bedrock: unweathered parent rock

CLORPT Factors

Climate — temperature & precipitation drive weathering rate
Organisms — plants, animals, microbes add organic matter
Lithology — parent rock mineralogy controls soil chemistry
Orography — slope affects drainage and erosion
Parent material — source rock composition
Time — older soils are more developed (thousands of years)

Weathering Types

Physical: freeze-thaw cycles, abrasion, exfoliation — breaks rock into smaller pieces without chemical change. Chemical: hydrolysis, oxidation, carbonation, dissolution — changes mineral composition. Biological: root wedging, burrowing animals, lichens — both physical and chemical action.
