Simple Machines Lab Interactive Lab · Less Force = More Distance · K5 Science
Key Formula
Work = Force × Distance. A machine doesn't save work — it lets you use less force over a longer path.
Ideal Mechanical Advantage = distance you push ÷ distance load moves.
Lab Goal

Drag the effort (blue ball) to apply force. Watch the load (red box) move. Adjust the lever arm length to see how mechanical advantage changes the force needed!

Try the Wheel & Axle to see how a big wheel turning a small axle multiplies your force.

The Screw & Wedge shows how spiral and angled shapes give you huge mechanical advantage.

Try This!
Move the fulcrum closer to the load. What happens to the force you need? Move it closer to the effort — what happens then?
Science Fact
In ancient Rome, cranes used compound pulleys with wheel-and-axle systems to lift huge stones for building the Colosseum!
Why It Matters
Every tool you use is a simple machine or combination of them: bicycles, scissors, screwdrivers, door hinges, and ramps at the grocery store.
