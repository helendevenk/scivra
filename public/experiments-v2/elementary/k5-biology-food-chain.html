Energy Transfer

Trophic Levels

🌱 Producers (Plants) — Convert sunlight into chemical energy via photosynthesis. Base of all food chains.
🐛 Primary Consumers — Herbivores that eat plants. (Rabbits, caterpillars, deer, grasshoppers)
🦊 Secondary Consumers — Carnivores that eat herbivores. (Foxes, frogs, small birds)
🦅 Tertiary Consumers — Top predators. (Eagles, wolves, sharks, humans)
🍄 Decomposers — Break down dead organisms, returning nutrients to soil. (Fungi, bacteria, worms)

10% Energy Rule

Only about 10% of energy is transferred from one trophic level to the next. The rest is lost as heat. This is why food chains rarely exceed 5 levels.

Why It Matters

Removing any species can cascade through the whole ecosystem. Wolves removed from Yellowstone changed river courses! Every organism matters.
