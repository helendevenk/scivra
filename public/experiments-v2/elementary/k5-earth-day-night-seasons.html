🌍 Day, Night & Seasons

K5 Earth Science — Grades 2-5 | Ages 7-11

Learning Objectives

●Explain why we have day and night (Earth's rotation)
●Describe why seasons occur (Earth's axial tilt)
●Identify the four seasons and solstices/equinoxes
●Understand the difference between rotation and revolution

Key Formulas

Day and Night

Earth rotates on its axis once every 24 hours. The side facing the Sun has day; the other side has night. The line between them is called the terminator.

Seasons

☀️ Summer Solstice (June 21) — Northern hemisphere tilted toward Sun. Longest day, highest Sun angle. ~15 hours daylight.
❄️ Winter Solstice (Dec 21) — Northern hemisphere tilted away from Sun. Shortest day. ~9 hours daylight.
🌸 Spring/Fall Equinox (Mar 20, Sep 22) — Earth's axis perpendicular to Sun direction. Equal day and night (~12 hours each).

Earth's Axial Tilt

Earth's axis is tilted at 23.5° relative to its orbital plane. This tilt — not distance from the Sun — causes seasons!

Why It Matters

Seasons determine crop growing seasons, animal migration patterns, holiday traditions, and influence global weather systems and climate zones.
