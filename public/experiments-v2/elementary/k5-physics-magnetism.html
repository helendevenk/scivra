Key Ideas
Opposite poles attract (N-S), same poles repel (N-N or S-S). The closer the magnets, the stronger the force!
Magnetic field strength decreases quickly with distance from the magnet.
What's Happening?

Every magnet has a North pole and a South pole. The magnetic field flows from North to South (you can see it with iron filings!).

An electromagnet is made by running electricity through a coil of wire. More current = stronger magnet. You can turn it on and off!

A compass needle always points to Earth's magnetic North Pole.

Fun Fact
The Earth itself is a giant magnet! Its iron core creates a magnetic field that protects us from harmful solar wind and guides compass needles.
Remember
You can never make a magnet with just one pole. If you cut a magnet in half, you get TWO smaller magnets, each with its own N and S pole!
Why It Matters
Magnets are in electric motors, speakers, MRI machines, credit cards, and hard drives. Electromagnets are used in junkyards to pick up cars!
