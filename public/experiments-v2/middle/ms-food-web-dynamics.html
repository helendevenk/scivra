Key Concepts

Keystone species: has outsized impact on ecosystem balance
Trophic cascade: removing a predator causes chain reaction through all levels
Invasive species: disrupts established food web relationships
Predator-prey oscillations: populations cycle out of phase

Yellowstone Wolves
Wolves removed 1926. Elk populations exploded → overgrazing → riverbanks eroded → fish disappeared. Wolves reintroduced 1995 → elk moved → vegetation recovered → rivers recovered. One keystone species changed everything.
Sea Otters (Kelp Forest)
Sea otters eat sea urchins. Without otters, urchins explode → eat kelp roots → kelp forest destroyed → fish, seals, eagles lose habitat. Otters as keystone species maintain entire ecosystem.
