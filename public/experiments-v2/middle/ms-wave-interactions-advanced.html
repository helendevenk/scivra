Wave Interactions
Learning Objectives
• Describe constructive and destructive interference patterns in 2D wave media
• Apply superposition principle: resultant displacement equals sum of individual wave displacements
• Identify nodal lines (zero displacement) and antinodal lines (maximum displacement)
• Relate wavelength, frequency, and wave speed through v = fλ
• Explain how phase difference determines interference type
Key Formulas
Wave Superposition
The Principle of Superposition states that when two or more waves overlap in the same medium, the resultant displacement at any point is the algebraic sum of the individual displacements.
Constructive Interference occurs when waves arrive in phase (Δφ = 0°, 360°, ...). The amplitudes add, creating regions of maximum displacement called antinodes.
Destructive Interference occurs when waves arrive out of phase (Δφ = 180°, 540°, ...). The amplitudes cancel, creating quiet regions called nodes.
Nodal Lines
Nodal lines are hyperbolic curves where path difference = (n + ½)λ for integer n. Points on these lines experience permanent zero displacement regardless of time, creating "dead zones" in the wave pattern.
Between nodal lines, antinodal lines follow path difference = nλ, where the two waves always reinforce each other.
