Concept

Every atom has a nucleus containing protons (positive) and neutrons (neutral), surrounded by electron shells. The atomic number Z defines the element. Shell capacities: shell 1 max 2, shell 2 max 8, shell 3 max 18. Isotopes have the same Z but different neutron counts. An ion has gained or lost electrons, giving it a net electric charge.

Real World
Carbon-14 (isotope) enables radiocarbon dating of fossils up to 50,000 years old. Iodine-131 treats thyroid cancer by targeting thyroid cells. MRI scanners exploit hydrogen proton spin in magnetic fields. Sodium (Z=11) loses one electron to form Na+ ions critical for nerve impulses.
Misconception Alert
Electrons do NOT travel in fixed circular paths. The Bohr model is a simplified teaching tool. Real electrons exist in quantum probability clouds called orbitals. Bohr correctly predicts energy levels for hydrogen but fails for heavier atoms, where quantum mechanics is required.
