Core Equations

Angular Momentum

Angular momentum L = Iω is a vector quantity pointing along the rotation axis (right-hand rule). For a rigid body, it equals the product of moment of inertia and angular velocity.

Torque & Precession

When an external torque acts perpendicular to L, it causes precession. The precession rate is Ω = τ/L = Mgr/(Iω). The L vector sweeps out a cone.

Conservation Law

When net torque = 0, angular momentum is conserved. A figure skater pulling arms in reduces I, so ω must increase to keep L = Iω constant.

Why It Matters (AP Physics C)

Angular momentum and its conservation appear on every AP Physics C exam. Understanding precession, gyroscopes, and rotational dynamics is essential for the free-response section. Connects to astrophysics (pulsar spin-up), engineering (gyrostabilizers).
