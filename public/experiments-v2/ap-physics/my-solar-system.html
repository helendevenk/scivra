Concept

In a system with more than 2 bodies, exact analytical solutions generally don't exist — this is the famous "three-body problem." Orbits can be stable, quasi-periodic, or chaotically unpredictable.

Total energy and angular momentum are conserved exactly (within numerical precision). If you see them drifting, that's integration error — a signal to use smaller time steps.

The center of mass moves at constant velocity (or stays fixed if initial CM momentum = 0). This is why our solar system's barycenter stays near the Sun even as planets orbit.

Knowledge Cards

Historical Fact

Poincaré proved in 1890 that the 3-body problem is generally non-integrable. His work founded the modern field of chaos theory — small perturbations produce exponentially diverging trajectories.

Common Misconception

More bodies doesn't always mean instability. Trojan asteroids at Jupiter's L4/L5 Lagrange points have been stable for billions of years despite being in a 3-body system.

Advanced Connection

The "figure-8" three-body orbit (discovered 1993, proved stable 2000) shows three equal masses chasing each other in a stable figure-8. These exotic solutions exist but require extremely precise initial conditions.
