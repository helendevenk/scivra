How It Works

Vectors have both magnitude (size) and direction. Adding two vectors is equivalent to placing them tail-to-head: the resultant R goes from the start of A to the end of B.

Switch between Head-to-Tail (chain rule) and Parallelogram methods using the buttons on the right. Both give the exact same resultant — they're just different geometric constructions that prove the same math.

Watch the data panel: when vectors are perpendicular, the dot product is zero. When they point the same direction, it equals |A||B|. The dot product measures the component of one vector along the direction of another.

Knowledge Cards

Why Components Work

Any vector can be decomposed into x and y components. Since x and y are perpendicular (independent), you can add x-components separately from y-components, then recombine. This is the power of Cartesian coordinates.

Common Misconception

|A + B| ≠ |A| + |B| in general. The triangle inequality states |A + B| ≤ |A| + |B|, with equality only when A and B point in exactly the same direction. Try the "Opposing" preset to see them cancel!

AP Connection

AP Physics 1 requires resolving forces into components, adding velocity vectors, and analyzing net force via vector diagrams. The dot product appears in AP Physics C (work = F·d·cosθ) and in wave interference analysis.
