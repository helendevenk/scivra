Circular Motion AP Physics · Centripetal Force & Acceleration
Core Equations
\(a_c = \frac{v^2}{r} = \omega^2 r\)
Centripetal acceleration — always directed toward center
\(F_c = \frac{mv^2}{r} = m\omega^2 r\)
Net centripetal force required for circular motion
\(T = \frac{2\pi r}{v} = \frac{2\pi}{\omega}\)
Period of one complete revolution
\(\omega = 2\pi f = \frac{v}{r}\)
Angular velocity (rad/s)
Scenario Formulas
Conical Pendulum
Tension components: T cos θ = mg (vertical), T sin θ = mv²/r (horizontal). Cone angle: tan θ = v²/(rg).
Banked Curve (frictionless)
tan θ = v²/(rg). At design speed no friction needed. N cos θ = mg, N sin θ = mv²/r.
Vertical Circle — Min Speed
At top: mg + N = mv²/r. Minimum speed when N = 0: v_min = √(rg). Below this the object falls off.
Apparent Weight

In circular motion, apparent weight = normal force N. At the bottom of a loop: N = m(g + v²/r) — you feel heavier. At the top: N = m(v²/r − g) — you feel lighter (or weightless at v_min).

The centripetal force is NOT a separate force — it is the net inward force provided by existing forces (tension, normal, gravity components).

Key Relationships
\(v_{min} = \sqrt{rg}\)
Minimum speed at top of vertical circle
\(\tan\theta = \frac{v^2}{rg}\)
