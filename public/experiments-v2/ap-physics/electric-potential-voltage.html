Concept

Electric potential V is a scalar (just a number, no direction), unlike the vector electric field E. This makes it much easier to add contributions from multiple charges.

Equipotential surfaces are regions where V is constant. E is always perpendicular to equipotential surfaces — and always points from high V to low V.

The height map in this visualization maps potential directly to elevation: high potential = tall peaks (red), low potential = deep valleys (blue).

Did You Know?
Scalar Advantage
Finding V from multiple charges is just addition of numbers. Finding E requires vector addition — much harder!
At Infinity
By convention, V = 0 at infinity. All potential values are measured relative to this reference point.
AP Connection
ΔV = −∫E·dr. Work done by field on charge q₀ moving from A to B: W = q₀(V_A − V_B).
Why It Matters
▸Voltage drives current in all electrical circuits
