Concept

Ohm's Law describes the linear relationship between voltage and current in ohmic conductors — materials where resistance stays constant regardless of applied voltage. The V-I graph of an ohmic resistor is a straight line; its slope is the resistance.

Not all materials obey Ohm's Law. A diode is a classic non-ohmic device: it allows current only in one direction and has an exponential V-I curve. Temperature also breaks linearity — as a filament heats up, its resistance rises, bending the V-I curve downward.

Slope = Resistance

On a V-I graph (V on x-axis, I on y-axis): slope = 1/R. A steeper line means lower resistance — more current per volt.

Temperature Coefficient

Copper: α ≈ 0.0039 /°C. Tungsten (filament): α ≈ 0.0045 /°C. Semiconductors have negative α — resistance drops with temperature.
