Concept

A DC circuit provides a continuous, one-directional path for charge carriers (electrons). The battery acts as an EMF source, maintaining a potential difference (voltage) that drives current through resistors. Current is the rate of charge flow — every electron that leaves the negative terminal must return through the positive terminal, making current the same everywhere in a series loop.

Resistors in series share the same current; resistors in parallel share the same voltage. The animated particles visualize conventional current flow from + to −.

Historical Insight

Georg Ohm published V=IR in 1827, initially ridiculed — his work was only recognized a decade later with the Royal Society's Copley Medal.

Common Misconception

Current is NOT "used up" by resistors. The same current flows through all series elements. Only energy (voltage) is consumed.
