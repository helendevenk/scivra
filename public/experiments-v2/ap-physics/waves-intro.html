Waves: Introduction AP Physics — Transverse vs Longitudinal
Core Formulas
Concept
Transverse waves — particles move perpendicular to wave travel direction. Light and water surface waves are examples.
Longitudinal waves — particles move parallel (back and forth) to wave travel. Sound in air is the classic example: compressions and rarefactions.
Both wave types obey v = λf. The wave speed depends on the medium, not on frequency or amplitude.
Key Facts
Amplitude vs Energy: Wave intensity scales as A². Doubling amplitude quadruples the energy transported per unit time.
Misconception: Particles do NOT travel with the wave. They oscillate locally around equilibrium while the wave pattern moves forward.
AP Connection: AP Physics 1 Unit 10. Must distinguish transverse vs longitudinal; understand λ = v/f; identify wavelength, period, amplitude from diagrams.
Why It Matters
  • Sound (longitudinal) vs light (transverse) — fundamental physics
  • Seismic P-waves (longitudinal) and S-waves (transverse)
  • Ultrasound imaging uses longitudinal pressure waves
