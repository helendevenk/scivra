CONCEPT
The atomic nucleus is a quantum battle between two forces. The strong nuclear force (range ~1–3 fm) attracts all nucleons. The Coulomb force repels protons at all distances. The nucleus exists only where strong force wins.

Binding energy per nucleon peaks at iron-56 (~8.79 MeV/nucleon). Nuclei lighter than iron can release energy by fusion; heavier nuclei release energy by fission. This is why both the Sun and nuclear bombs work.

Magic numbers (2, 8, 20, 28, 50, 82, 126) arise from filled nuclear shells — like noble gases in chemistry but for nucleons.
DID YOU KNOW
⚛️ Protons are only ~0.85 fm in radius. A nucleus the size of a marble would make an atom the size of a football stadium.
⚠️ Misconception: neutrons aren't just "fillers". They dilute Coulomb repulsion among protons while adding strong force attraction.
