Concept

Electric field lines emerge from positive charges and terminate on negative charges. They never cross — at every point, the field has a unique direction.

The density of field lines represents field strength: lines packed closely together indicate a strong field, widely spaced lines indicate a weak field.

By the superposition principle, the total field at any point is the vector sum of contributions from all charges.

Did You Know?
Michael Faraday
Faraday invented the concept of field lines in the 1830s as a visual tool — before Maxwell formalized them mathematically.
Key Rule
Field lines never cross. If two lines crossed at a point, the field would have two directions there — physically impossible.
AP Connection
Gauss's Law: the net flux through a closed surface = Q_enc/ε₀. Field lines visualize flux directly.
