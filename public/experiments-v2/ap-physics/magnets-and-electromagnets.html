Concept
Magnetic fields arise from moving charges. In a solenoid, the field is nearly uniform inside — n turns per meter carrying current I produces B = μ₀nI. Iron cores dramatically amplify this by aligning magnetic domains.
A permanent bar magnet behaves like a magnetic dipole. Field strength falls as 1/r³ along the axis — much faster than gravity (1/r²). Magnetic monopoles do not exist: field lines always form closed loops.
Knowledge Cards
🧲 μ₀ = 4π×10⁻⁷ T·m/A — permeability of free space. Iron core: μ ≈ 5000 μ₀, amplifying B enormously.
⚠️ Misconception — Field lines don't show force direction on a moving charge. Use F = qv × B instead.
📡 AP Connection — AP Physics C E&M Unit 3. Ampere's Law derivation of solenoid field is a core topic.
Why It Matters
• Electric motors and generators — every one uses a solenoid
• MRI: superconducting solenoids at 1.5–3 T
• Hard drives store bits via magnetic domains on spinning platters
