Balancing Act AP Physics · Rotational Equilibrium · Στ = 0
Core Equations
\(\tau = r \times F = rF\sin\theta\)
Torque = lever arm × force (N·m). Counterclockwise positive.
\(\sum \tau = 0\)
Rotational equilibrium condition: net torque about any pivot = 0
\(m_1 d_1 = m_2 d_2\)
Seesaw balance (perpendicular gravity forces, equal lever arms)
\(\tau_{net} = I\alpha\)
Rotational Newton's 2nd Law — unbalanced torque causes angular acceleration
Key Concepts
Lever Arm
Perpendicular distance from pivot to line of action of force. A longer lever arm amplifies torque for the same force magnitude.
Center of Mass
Point where gravity effectively acts. Object tips when CG moves outside base of support. Stable objects: low CG, wide base.
Real Applications
Cranes: counterweight τ balances load τ. Bridges: distributed loads sum to zero. Human posture: spine shifts to keep CG over feet.
Meter Stick Problems
Choose pivot at unknown force to eliminate it from Στ=0. Then solve for remaining unknowns using one torque equation.
Tipping Point

An object tips when its center of gravity moves outside its base of support. At the tipping point, the weight vector passes through the edge — any further shift causes irreversible rotation.
