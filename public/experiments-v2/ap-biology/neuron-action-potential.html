Neuron Action Potential Na⁺/K⁺ Channels · AP Biology
Core Equations
Nernst equation: equilibrium potential for ion X across membrane.
Goldman equation: resting potential from all permeable ions (PK≫PNa at rest).
Na⁺/K⁺ ATPase: 3 Na⁺ out, 2 K⁺ in per ATP — restores gradients.
Action Potential Phases
① Resting (-70 mV): K⁺ leak channels open; Na⁺/K⁺ pump maintains gradient.
② Threshold (-55 mV): Sufficient depolarization opens voltage-gated Na⁺ channels.
③ Depolarization (+40 mV): Na⁺ rushes in; membrane becomes positive.
④ Repolarization: Na⁺ channels inactivate; K⁺ channels open fully.
⑤ Hyperpolarization (-80 mV): K⁺ overshoot; membrane briefly more negative than rest.
⑥ Refractory Period: Na⁺ channels inactivated — absolute refractory. Then relative refractory as K⁺ channels close.

Common Misconception
The action potential is NOT a flow of electricity — it is a propagating wave of ion channel openings. Individual ions move just nanometers. Conduction speed (up to 120 m/s in myelinated axons) reflects how fast the wave propagates, not how fast ions move.
Why It Matters
Local anesthetics (lidocaine) block voltage-gated Na⁺ channels — preventing action potential propagation. Multiple sclerosis destroys myelin, slowing saltatory conduction. Tetrodotoxin (puffer fish) specifically blocks Na⁺ channels with picomolar affinity.
