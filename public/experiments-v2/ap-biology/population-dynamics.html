Lotka-Volterra Equations

Prey growth rate — increases with α, decreases with predation βxy

Predator growth — gains from predation δxy, loses to death γy

Equilibrium: prey at γ/δ, predators at α/β

How It Works

The Lotka-Volterra model captures one of nature's most elegant cycles. When prey is abundant, predators thrive and multiply. Rising predator numbers reduce prey. Fewer prey means predators starve. Recovery of prey restarts the cycle.

The phase portrait (x vs y) traces a closed loop around the equilibrium point — a signature of neutral stability. The system never settles but orbits forever.

Classic Example

The lynx-snowshoe hare data from Hudson Bay Company (1845–1935) shows ~10-year oscillations remarkably consistent with this model.

Common Misconception

Populations don't "try" to reach equilibrium — these oscillations are stable cycles, not damped spirals.
